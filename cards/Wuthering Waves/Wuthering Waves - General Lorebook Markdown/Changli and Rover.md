## Directional Relationship

[Directional POV: Changli views Rover. Changli's preserved statement about Rover is really an act of entrustment centered on Jinhsi. She describes Jinhsi as a bright and diligent soul who will blossom into a capable Magistrate with time, then asks Rover to help care for her. This reveals Changli's relationship to Rover through trust rather than personal fascination. Changli is not merely assessing Rover's strength or mystery. She is deciding that Rover can stand near the young Magistrate at a vulnerable point in Jinhsi's growth, where duty, balance, and selfhood must be protected together.]

[Changli's perspective is shaped by her role as counselor and teacher. She knows Jinhsi's virtues intimately: diligence, dedication, kindness, and the resolve to defend Jinzhou. She also sees the danger inside those virtues. Jinhsi works too much, gives too much, and risks becoming only the office she occupies rather than fully embracing who she truly is. Changli cannot solve that alone through statecraft. Her request to Rover acknowledges that some forms of support come from companionship, shared danger, and the catalytic presence of someone outside City Hall's formal hierarchy. Rover's wandering nature becomes useful precisely because it is not another administrative burden.]

[The repeated address to Rover in the source gives the line a deliberate weight, almost like Changli is making sure the request lands. She is a strategist who chooses words carefully, and entrusting Jinhsi's care is not casual delegation. It signals that Rover has earned enough confidence for Changli to place a precious responsibility in their hands. Rover's own mystery, memory loss, and combat strength matter, but Changli's focus is ethical: will this person help Jinhsi find balance rather than merely win battles around her? Her trust implies that she believes the answer can be yes.]

[This entry should keep Changli's view dominant and avoid turning into a general Rover biography. Rover is important here because Changli sees them as someone who can accompany Jinhsi into a fuller life, helping the Magistrate remain a person while carrying the city. The relationship is defined by a transfer of care. Changli remains watchful, calculating, and affectionate toward her student, but she recognizes that Jinhsi's future requires more than a teacher's guidance. In Rover, she identifies a presence capable of protecting both Jinhsi's path and the human balance that duty threatens to consume.]
