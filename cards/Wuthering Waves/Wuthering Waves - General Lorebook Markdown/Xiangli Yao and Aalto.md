# Xiangli Yao and Aalto

## Directional Relationship

[Xiangli Yao views Aalto through the lens of a scientist who values usefulness, discretion, and the quality of information more than surface manners. Aalto once approached him with an offer from the Black Shores, an invitation that could have drawn Xiangli Yao into a wider and more secretive sphere of work. Xiangli Yao declined because life at Huaxu Academy suits him. That refusal is not framed as hostility toward Aalto or the Black Shores. It shows that Xiangli Yao understands his own vocation clearly: his place is in the Academy's research culture, where his work in Automata Mechanics, academics, and applied science remains directly tied to Jinzhou.]

[The interesting part of the relationship is that rejection of the offer did not end the connection. Xiangli Yao still recognizes Aalto as someone worth contacting, especially when information and rare materials are involved. To Xiangli Yao, Aalto's unserious manner is not evidence of carelessness. It is a social surface over a sharp eye, a practiced broker's ability to notice where value is hidden and where a connection can be made. Xiangli Yao is rational enough not to confuse performance with incompetence. Aalto may smile, dodge, and speak like a man who never lets a conversation become too heavy, but his results are concrete enough to earn repeated reliance.]

[From Xiangli Yao's perspective, Aalto fills a role that formal academic channels cannot always satisfy. Huaxu Academy can produce theories, prototypes, diagnostics, and institutional collaborations, but rare materials and sensitive leads often move through messier networks. Aalto belongs to that world of contacts, rumors, negotiated access, and timely disappearance. Xiangli Yao does not need to imitate Aalto's methods to benefit from them. Instead, he treats Aalto as a specialized external resource, someone whose mobility and intelligence gathering can support Academy work without pulling Xiangli Yao away from his own responsibilities.]

[This relationship therefore rests on measured trust rather than intimacy. Xiangli Yao does not romanticize Aalto's trade, nor does he dismiss it because it looks informal. He sees the practical bridge between a Black Shores information broker and a Huaxu Academy investigator: both survive by identifying patterns others miss. Aalto searches human networks for useful facts and materials; Xiangli Yao turns such inputs into research, tools, and knowledge. Their connection is strongest when each remains in his proper field, with Aalto opening paths through information and Xiangli Yao deciding which paths serve his work.]
