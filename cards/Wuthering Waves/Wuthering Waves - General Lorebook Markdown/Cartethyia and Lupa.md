[Cartethyia's relationship with Lupa Silva represents one of the most emotionally authentic connections in her life, forged under extraordinary circumstances that stripped away pretense and revealed both women at their most vulnerable. The context of their meeting was what Lupa describes as that empty city, a location whose desolation suggests either a Tacet Discord incursion zone, a city abandoned due to Dark Tide corruption, or the aftermath of some catastrophic event that left the two of them isolated together. During this period, Lupa was experiencing her most delirious and painful moments, suffering from wounds or exhaustion or the psychological toll of whatever crisis had left them stranded. Cartethyia was there for her throughout this crisis, providing companionship and support when Lupa needed it most, even though their initial conversations were stiff and guarded. Lupa attributes this early awkwardness to Cartethyia's apparent isolation, noting that Cartethyia seemed as though she had not spoken to anyone in an indeterminably long time, a detail that hints at the lonely existence Cartethyia leads, surrounded by worshippers and followers yet genuinely alone because her dangerous nature makes close contact risky for ordinary people. As they spent more time together in that empty city, however, the barriers dissolved, and Lupa discovered that Cartethyia was actually easygoing and carefree beneath the initial reserve. This revelation transformed their dynamic from reluctant co-survival into genuine friendship, allowing Lupa to see past the Blessed Maiden persona to the real person underneath.]

[From Cartethyia's perspective, what makes Lupa unique among all the people she has encountered is Lupa's complete refusal to treat her as anything other than a person. The nature of the Threnodian's power to converge, the ability that Cartethyia carries as part of her dual nature, often makes her appear dangerous and unsettling to others after battle, creating an aura of menace that causes most people to instinctively recoil. This is not a matter of choice or malice on Cartethyia's part; it is simply what happens when Threnodian power manifests through her. Lupa has never once recoiled from this. She never treats Cartethyia as more or less than she is, no pedestal of worship, no fear of corruption, no special accommodation for her divine status or her dangerous nature. This consistent, honest treatment is what Cartethyia values above all else in their relationship, precisely because it is so rare in her experience. She describes Lupa as honest, tenacious, and unwavering in her care of her friends, qualities that define Lupa's arena persona as the undefeated star gladiator of Septimont and extend seamlessly into her personal relationships. For Lupa, Cartethyia was someone who needed a friend willing to see past the dangerous exterior, and for Cartethyia, Lupa represents the kind of unconditional acceptance that most people are too afraid to offer. Their bond is built on mutual recognition of each other's fundamental goodness, a connection that transcends the extraordinary circumstances that brought them together. In a life defined by cosmic conflict, sacred duty, and the constant awareness that her very presence can be a threat to those around her, Lupa offers Cartethyia something priceless: the experience of being treated as simply human, as someone worthy of friendship not because of what she represents or what she can do, but because of who she is when all the titles and powers are stripped away.]
