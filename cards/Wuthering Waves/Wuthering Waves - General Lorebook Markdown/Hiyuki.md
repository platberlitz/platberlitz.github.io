# Hiyuki

## Identity

[Hiyuki is a Resonator of Glacio frequency who fights with a Sword, a severe and sacred figure whose official classification remains unclear even though her public and institutional identities are unusually dense. She is known by the title Futures' Tithe and by the names Ghost Miko, Lady Miko, and Miko of Eternal Flaming Sakura, each title emphasizing a different part of her role: a bearer of possible futures, a shrine maiden marked by distance and survival, a formal religious figure, and the continuing vessel of an Ashinoharan tradition that should have vanished with its homeland. Her origin is Ashinohara, while her present national context is Lahai-Roi, a region defined by Startorch Academy, the Spacetrek Collective, the Roya Frostlands, Exostrider, and conflicts surrounding the Threnodian Aleph-1. Hiyuki is not merely a visiting relic from another culture. She is an active member of Lahai-Roi's present crisis structure, a miko and Special Response Force operative whose spiritual duty, combat service, and institutional obligations converge around threats that ordinary authorities cannot easily contain.]

[The core tension in Hiyuki's identity is that she belongs to both a lost past and an unstable present. Ashinohara is described as her birthplace and as a vanished land tied to miko traditions, entrusted wishes, and the unresolved mystery of its disappearance. Lahai-Roi, by contrast, is the field where those inherited burdens become operational duties. She carries Ashinohara's memory into a country whose own northern conflicts involve Aleph-1, Startorch, the Frostlands, and abnormal resonance phenomena. This makes Hiyuki a living bridge between traditions: the ceremonial world of the Miko of Flaming Sakura, the scientific and classified world of the Spacetrek Collective, the institutional world of Startorch Academy, and the emergency response world of the Special Response Force.]

## Appearance

[Hiyuki's appearance is austere, memorable, and closely aligned with the atmosphere of icy purity that surrounds her record. She is a tall albino woman with fair skin, long white hair, white eyelashes, and vivid red eyes. The contrast between pale hair and red irises gives her face a sharp, almost ceremonial intensity, as if her body visually announces both Glacio resonance and sacred danger. She has a small beauty mark beneath her left eye and a larger one on her right shoulder, details that prevent her severe image from becoming anonymous or abstract. Her Tacet Mark is located on the outer side of her upper right thigh, a placement that marks her as a Resonator while remaining separate from the face and hands that most immediately define her public presence.]

[The source describes her pale coloration and severe bearing as producing a striking, blade-like presence. That phrase is important because Hiyuki's visual design is not merely delicate or ghostly; it is edged. The whiteness of her hair and lashes, the clean severity of her posture, and the red intensity of her eyes combine into an impression of restraint sharpened into force. As Ghost Miko, she appears distant and almost spectral. As Lady Miko, she appears dignified and formal. As a swordswoman, however, the same features read as disciplined lethality. Her appearance therefore supports all sides of her identity at once: sacred survivor, Glacio Resonator, miko, and combatant.]

## Forte

[Hiyuki's recorded Forte or associated power is Futures' Tithe, a name that directly connects her ability to possibility, consequence, and sacrifice. The most concrete explanation of her anomalous capabilities comes through Suzu, one of the five Legacy Talismans left behind by the Sentinels. Having served as a miko for over a century, Hiyuki derives her most unusual power from this talisman, which grants its bearer the ability to perceive and utilize certain possibilities of oneself. In practical terms, the record frames her not as someone who simply freezes enemies with Glacio force, but as a Resonator whose sacred tool allows contact with alternative potentials. The title Futures' Tithe suggests that such power is not free. To draw on possible selves is to owe something to time, duty, or fate, and Hiyuki's long service implies that she has paid that cost repeatedly.]

[The Spacetrek Collective's classified interview dossier on Hiyuki is itself incomplete, stating that a truly comprehensive evaluation was foregone because of multifaceted considerations. That omission is useful evidence. Institutions in Lahai-Roi can measure many Resonator phenomena, yet Hiyuki resists a full public account, either because her sacred position complicates scientific access, because Suzu and the Sentinels fall outside normal Forte categories, or because her connection to Aleph-1 makes complete disclosure dangerous. What is available is still technically significant. Her Rabelle's Waveform diagram exhibits an elliptical oscillation pattern with a stable Time Domain representation. No abnormal fluctuations were observed, her examination results remain within normal parameters, her current Overclocking threshold is exceptionally high, and her subject stability is robust. Hiyuki is therefore not portrayed as volatile despite the strangeness of Suzu. She is a deeply controlled Resonator whose power touches dangerous possibilities while her waveform remains stable enough to sustain long service, field duty, and repeated confrontation with abnormal threats.]

## Personality

[Hiyuki's personality is summarized through the phrase embodiment of icy purity, but the surrounding details make that purity active rather than passive. She is not cold because she lacks feeling. She is cold because her feelings have been disciplined into service. The wishes entrusted to her in brief encounters with others drive her onward, carving a path forward through duty rather than impulse. The language of entrusted wishes echoes Ashinohara's vanished traditions and the Miko of Flaming Sakura's role, suggesting that Hiyuki understands even small meetings as spiritually consequential. People pass through her life, leave hopes or burdens with her, and those fragments become part of the force that keeps her moving.]

[Her bearing is severe, yet the history facet shows that she is capable of grief, shock, and tightly held pain. When she first heard the truth of her own past from Gyokuro's drunken words, her mouth tightened and she answered not with an outburst but with silence and bitter liquor. That reaction is consistent with a woman trained to contain herself, even when confronted with revelations that strike at her origin and role. Hiyuki's restraint should not be mistaken for indifference. Her long life, her inherited duty, and her connection to a vanished homeland seem to have made her careful with emotion because emotion has weight. She carries wishes, guilt, sacred obligations, and classified responsibilities, and her purity is the discipline required to keep carrying them without breaking.]

## History

[Hiyuki's history begins in Ashinohara, a birthplace now described as vanished and mysterious. The land is associated with miko traditions, wishes entrusted across time, Suzu, long life, and the disappearance that left its memory dependent on survivors and sacred offices. As Miko of Flaming Sakura, Hiyuki appears to be one of the central carriers of that memory. The tradition belongs to Ashinohara and to her sacred duty, and it includes inherited wishes, service as a miko, the talisman Suzu, and the burden of confronting Aleph-1. The fact that she has served as a miko for over a century makes her history unusually long in human terms. She is not a newly appointed shrine maiden learning an inherited role. She is the accumulated result of generations of duty compressed into one living figure, a woman whose personal life has become inseparable from the survival of a lost culture's spiritual mandate.]

[The available history record is fragmentary but revealing. It repeats that Hiyuki is a Special Response Force member and that she has a long-standing connection to the Threnodian Aleph-1, then preserves a section titled Words Left Unsaid. In that fragment, the first time Hiyuki hears the truth of her own past, it comes from Gyokuro's drunken lips. Her response is controlled and painful: her mouth tightens, she speaks no immediate answer, and she pours herself bitter liquor. The record then breaks around an address to Hiyuki and a reference to Rover being unable to become the Miko of Flaming Sakura. Because the source cuts off mid-thought, the exact revelation cannot be reconstructed with certainty. What can be said is that Hiyuki's past contains a concealed truth, that her miko role cannot simply be transferred to another person, and that the emotional weight of that truth is treated as something she absorbs in silence rather than resolves through explanation.]

[Her history therefore reads less like a sequence of public achievements and more like a burdened continuity. Ashinohara disappears, yet its miko tradition remains in her. Suzu comes from the Sentinels, yet its possibilities are filtered through her own selfhood. Aleph-1 belongs to Lahai-Roi's present conflicts, yet Hiyuki's connection to it is long-standing enough to feel like destiny rather than assignment. She moves through the present as a person already shaped by more than a century of service, and the record's gaps only strengthen the impression that some parts of her life are deliberately withheld, classified, or too painful to state directly.]

## Affiliations

[Hiyuki's affiliations are Miko of Flaming Sakura, Special Response Force, Spacetrek Collective, and Startorch Academy, with Ashinohara and Lahai-Roi providing the most important place context. The Miko of Flaming Sakura is her sacred identity, rooted in Ashinohara and bound to inherited wishes, Suzu, long service, and the burden of confronting Aleph-1. The Special Response Force is her operational identity within Lahai-Roi, where its work concerns abnormal threats, Aleph-1, Startorch, the Frostlands, and Hiyuki's combat duty as a miko. The Spacetrek Collective provides a classified research frame for her Forte and records, but its inability or refusal to publish a comprehensive evaluation shows the limits of ordinary institutional analysis when faced with Suzu, Sentinel legacy, and a miko whose power involves possible selves. Startorch Academy connects her to Lahai-Roi's broader system of Resonator care, research, medical work, teaching, Exostrider Engineering, and post-Lament survival.]

[These affiliations do not dilute Hiyuki's identity; they clarify why she is difficult to categorize. To Ashinohara, she is a living remnant and sacred office. To Lahai-Roi, she is an operative in conflicts involving abnormal threats and Aleph-1. To Startorch Academy and the Spacetrek Collective, she is a subject of Resonator knowledge who also exceeds normal evaluation. To herself, as far as the record allows, she is someone driven forward by wishes entrusted to her in brief encounters. Hiyuki's place in the lore is therefore not defined by a single institution or relationship. She is the point where vanished homeland, Sentinel inheritance, Glacio resonance, classified research, emergency duty, and Threnodian conflict meet in one austere figure.]
