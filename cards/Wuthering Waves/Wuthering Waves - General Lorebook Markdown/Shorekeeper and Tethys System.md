SELECTIVE

[Shorekeeper and Tethys System: Shorekeeper views Tethys System: Shorekeeper's relationship with the Tethys System is one of purpose, dependence, and cost. Tethys is the Black Shores' predictive and analytical platform for monitoring Lament threats, drawing together resonance readings, Tacet Discord activity, environmental frequency changes, and historical catastrophe data into guidance for action. For Shorekeeper, this system is not a distant tool. It is bound to the center of her existence. She can guide Tethys and act through its analysis, but that same massive computation wears against the Remnant Energy crystals that compose her body.]

[Shorekeeper does not understand Tethys only as cold machinery. She treats its selections as meaningful enough to contemplate, especially when they involve someone like Encore, whose imagination, radiance, and ability to dispel dismay do not look like conventional strategic assets. If Tethys saw something worth choosing in such a child, then Shorekeeper can read that choice as evidence that the system's function reaches beyond sterile arithmetic. It searches for patterns of survival, and survival may include qualities that cannot be reduced to weapons, rank, or predictive accuracy alone.]

[This relationship is therefore both intimate and impersonal. Tethys gives the Black Shores the ability to perceive threats no single mind could process, and Shorekeeper gives Tethys a guardian capable of interpreting, guiding, and enduring its burden. The system helps protect humanity, but its data load contributes to Shorekeeper's energy loss and crystal fragmentation. She replaces broken parts of herself and continues the work, which makes the bond feel almost symbiotic. Tethys calculates; Shorekeeper bears. Tethys selects; Shorekeeper wonders what the selection means. From her perspective, the system is not merely infrastructure, not merely command support, and not merely a sacred duty. It is the analytical heart of the Black Shores, a heart she serves even when its beating damages the body that serves it.]
