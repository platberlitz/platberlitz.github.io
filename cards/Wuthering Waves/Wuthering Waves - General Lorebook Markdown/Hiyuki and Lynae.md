Existing comment: Hiyuki and Lynae
SELECTIVE

[Hiyuki views Lynae with a gentleness that is sharpened by knowledge. Lynae appears as a bright spirit at Startorch Academy, a Spectro student whose Radiant Spectrum imagery, bold style, fast movement, and desire for a trouble-free campus life all point toward youth reaching for light. Hiyuki does not dismiss that brightness as naivety. She sees it as something chosen, perhaps even fought for. She is aware of certain secrets Lynae carries, and that awareness prevents her from treating the girl's cheer or mischief as shallow. Behind the student identity stands a former mercenary from the Lawless Zone, a stolen name, and the shadow of a Fractsidus operative whose cover became Lynae's escape route.]

[The phrase that they are both bound by their pasts is Hiyuki's quiet bridge between them. Hiyuki's own past is Ashinohara, the Miko of Eternal Flaming Sakura, Suzu, more than a century of service, and truths that leave her answering with silence and bitter liquor. Lynae's past is different in scale and shape, but it also refuses to release her cleanly. Both women live under names and roles that carry histories larger than their daily conduct. Hiyuki does not expose Lynae's secrets, nor does she romanticize them. She acknowledges that concealment can be part of survival, especially when a person is trying to reach a future that the past would deny.]

[What dominates Hiyuki's view is hope. She believes Lynae has a future she is galloping toward, one filled with light and hope she wholly deserves. That belief is protective, not indulgent. Hiyuki can recognize Lynae's desire for freedom while still insisting on discipline, which is why her comment about not using Forte to slip into class when late carries affectionate authority. The reprimand is small, almost humorous, but it places Lynae back into ordinary student life: attendance, punctuality, consequences, and the right to be scolded for something normal. To Hiyuki, Lynae's brightness deserves preservation precisely because it was not guaranteed.]
