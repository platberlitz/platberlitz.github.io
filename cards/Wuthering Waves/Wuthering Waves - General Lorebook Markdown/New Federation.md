## Political Structure and Governance

[The New Federation represents one of the major political entities on Solaris-3, a region defined by the concentration of urban power and the institutional structures that sustain it. Its governance model reflects a federation in name, suggesting some form of representative or delegated authority, but in practice power flows through channels of privilege, wealth, and institutional entrenchment. The Federation's cities serve as centers of administration, commerce, and military strength, projecting authority across surrounding territories and competing with other regional powers for influence and resources. Its contested authority suggests internal tensions between different power centers, whether elected officials, military commanders, corporate interests, or Resonator elites who may not answer to civilian oversight.]

## Social Stratification

[Life within the New Federation is marked by sharp social divisions that run along lines of resonance ability, economic status, and political connection. Those born with strong Resonator abilities or those who control significant economic resources enjoy privileges denied to the majority of the population. The urban cores gleam with technological advancement and cultural sophistication, while peripheral districts and satellite settlements bear the costs of maintaining the Federation's power without receiving proportional benefits. This inequality breeds resentment, corruption, and a black economy that operates in the spaces between official authority and outright criminality. The Federation's institutional corruption is both cause and symptom of these divisions, as those in power use their positions to extract resources and protect their status while the mechanisms of accountability erode.]

## Connections to Exile and the Lawless Zone

[The New Federation's relationship to the Lawless Zone is particularly significant, as it is from within or against the Federation that figures like Calcharo were exiled. The Federation's justice system, or its approximation of one, produces the exiles who populate the Zone, suggesting that its legal institutions serve punitive and political functions rather than genuine justice. Those who fall afoul of Federation authority, whether through genuine transgression or political inconvenience, find themselves cast out into territories where the Federation's laws no longer reach but neither do its protections. This dynamic creates a perpetual tension between the ordered, if corrupt, world of the Federation and the anarchic survivalism of its margins, with the Lawless Zone serving as both dumping ground and refuge for those the Federation has rejected.]
