## Directional Relationship

[Directional POV: Cantarella views Rosemary. Cantarella gives Rosemary the same sea-creature framing used for Gardenia: a lively Starfish and a gentle Sea Bunny, paired with the teasing claim that she cannot tell them apart. Rosemary's place in Cantarella's life is especially layered because existing context ties her both to companionship and to Fisalia apothecary work. A report attributed to Rosemary examines Cantarella's rare Sea of Dreams toxin, treating the matriarch's Forte as the pinnacle of the family's poison-related inheritance. From Cantarella's side, Rosemary is therefore intimate, useful, observant, and dangerously close to the truth of her body.]

[Cantarella's playful confusion softens a relationship that could otherwise feel clinical or unequal. Rosemary has access to the language of checkups, toxins, gardens, research permissions, and the medical record of a woman whose mind and body were damaged by trials and prolonged dream-poison use. That access could make Rosemary frightening, because knowledge is power in Porto-Veno Castle. Instead, Cantarella frames her through a whimsical aquatic image. Whether Rosemary is the gentle Sea Bunny or the lively Starfish matters less than the fact that Cantarella chooses not to pin her in place. Ambiguity becomes a way to keep affection, authority, and secrecy in balance.]

[From Cantarella's perspective, Rosemary's loyalty is not decorative. The Fisalia matriarch lives at the center of a lineage where poison, noble obligation, servants, gardens, and sacred history converge. Someone near her must understand the hazards of proximity: altered perception, the Sea of Dreams, the emotional cost of caring for a person whose kindness and threat are interwoven. Rosemary's companionship carries that burden quietly. Cantarella's teasing suggests that she recognizes the support but prefers not to name it too solemnly. A solemn name would make the dependence too exposed, while a joke lets her accept Rosemary's presence without surrendering the poise expected of the Bane.]

[This dossier should keep the relationship directional and centered on Cantarella's guarded affection. Rosemary is not simply summarized as an apothecary or companion; she is Rosemary as Cantarella sees her, a familiar figure in the dream-sea orbit of Fisalia, half distinct and half merged with Gardenia in the matriarch's playful language. The dynamic is intimate but veiled. Cantarella may joke that she cannot tell them apart, yet the joke itself reveals how necessary the pair has become to the private ecosystem of her castle, her health, and her fragile search for liberation from the family legacy.]
