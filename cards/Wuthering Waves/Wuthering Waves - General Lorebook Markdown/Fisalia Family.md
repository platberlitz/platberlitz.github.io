## Power and Legacy

[The Fisalia family stands among the most powerful and feared noble houses of Ragunna, their name synonymous with both aristocratic prestige and the art of poison. Their influence extends through political connections, economic leverage, and the particular expertise in toxicology that has become their signature. The family's castle, described as crown-like and perched atop a mountain, physically embodies their elevated status and separation from those beneath them both socially and literally. Within Ragunna's complex power structure, the Fisalia name carries weight that opens doors and closes mouths, a reputation built over generations of careful maneuvering and the quiet elimination of obstacles.]

## The Sea of Dreams

[The Fisalia family is connected to something called the Sea of Dreams, a phenomenon that appears to be both a metaphysical space and a tool in the family's arsenal. Current family head Cantarella resides in her mountain castle where illusory dreams flow like streams, meticulously spun by her own hands, suggesting that the Sea of Dreams is not merely inherited but actively maintained and crafted by those who master its secrets. This dream-weaving capability represents a form of power that operates beyond conventional politics or combat, allowing the Fisalia to shape perception, memory, and desire in ways that leave no physical trace. The burdens carried by family members suggest that mastery of this domain comes at significant personal cost, a price that the family's public image of elegance and danger only partially conceals.]

## Cantarella, the Bane

[Cantarella, the current head of Fisalia, known as the Bane, represents the culmination of her family's traditions in a figure whose beauty is described as captivating and perilous in equal measure. As a Havoc Rectifier wielder, she channels the destructive dark frequency through a scholarly weapon, suggesting that her combat style reflects the same precision and subtlety that defines her family's approach to poison. Her position as family head places her at the intersection of noble obligation, personal ambition, and the supernatural responsibilities of maintaining the Sea of Dreams. The burdens she carries are both the weight of her family's legacy and the particular costs of the power she wields, making her simultaneously one of Ragunna's most dangerous figures and one of its most tragic.]
