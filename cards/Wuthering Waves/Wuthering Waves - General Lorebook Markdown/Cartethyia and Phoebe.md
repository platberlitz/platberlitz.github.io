Existing comment: Cartethyia and Phoebe
SELECTIVE

[Cartethyia's relationship with Phoebe is defined by deep respect, genuine affection, and the painful awareness of a truth that Cartethyia finds difficult to reveal. Phoebe possesses a kind and gentle heart, approaching her duties as an acolyte of the Order not as obligations to be fulfilled but as opportunities to serve others with compassion. Beyond her official duties, Phoebe always does her best to be there for others, offering emotional support and spiritual guidance to those who seek it, demonstrating the kind of authentic faith that transcends mere institutional loyalty and becomes a genuine force for good in people's lives. Cartethyia recognizes and treasures this quality in Phoebe, understanding that such pure-hearted devotion is rare even among those who serve the Order professionally. However, this admiration is complicated by Cartethyia's honest self-assessment: when it comes to knowledge of the Order's doctrines, she freely admits that she is nowhere near as good as Phoebe. This is not false modesty but accurate observation, Phoebe has dedicated herself to studying and understanding the theological foundations of Imperator worship, the sacred texts, the ritual practices, and the philosophical underpinnings of the Blessed Maiden tradition in ways that Cartethyia, who lived the role rather than studied it, never did.]

[This disparity in doctrinal knowledge creates a profound dilemma for Cartethyia regarding what she knows about the Order's true nature and what she feels obligated to reveal to its faithful members. Because of so many true and faithful believers like Phoebe, people whose devotion is genuine, whose service is selfless, and whose faith provides real comfort and meaning to their lives, Cartethyia finds it difficult to reveal the truth to the people of Ragunna about what lies behind the Order's sacred facade. The truth she refers to likely concerns the real nature of the Blessed Maiden role, the actual relationship between Imperator and the Threnodian forces, or perhaps the political manipulation that has corrupted the Order's original mission. Cartethyia understands that people turn to faith for many reasons, not just out of devotion to the Sentinel, some seek comfort in grief, others seek community in isolation, still others seek meaning in suffering, and destroying their faith would not automatically provide better alternatives for these needs. This awareness makes her hesitant to shatter the beliefs that sustain people like Phoebe, even though she knows that the institution they serve is more complicated and perhaps more compromised than they realize. Her protective instinct toward Phoebe's faith reveals Cartethyia's understanding that truth is not always the highest good, that sometimes preserving someone's hope and purpose matters more than exposing every corruption, and that the decision to reveal or conceal must be made with full awareness of the consequences for those who receive the information. For Phoebe, Cartethyia likely represents the living embodiment of everything she believes in, the Blessed Maiden made flesh, the connection between humanity and the divine made visible. She probably has no idea that Cartethyia's experience of that role was far more complex and painful than theological education could ever convey, or that Cartethyia now carries doubts about the very institution that Phoebe serves so faithfully. This asymmetry of knowledge creates a relationship where Cartethyia must constantly navigate between honesty and protection, between her desire to treat Phoebe as an equal who deserves to know the truth and her fear that revealing that truth would destroy something beautiful and irreplaceable in Phoebe's life.]
