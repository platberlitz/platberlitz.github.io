[Mortefi and Chixia: Mortefi views Chixia: Mortefi's view of Chixia is warmer than his usual guarded judgments because she reaches him through straightforward conduct rather than academic display. She is a regular visitor at his lab, often bringing children along, which means he experiences her not as an abstract Public Security Bureau Patroller but as a recurring presence in the practical space where his research, tools, and routines are usually protected from frivolity. That could have irritated him. Mortefi values order, focus, and respect for expertise. Yet Chixia's manner disarms the irritation because she is direct, energetic, and transparent in a way that does not feel manipulative.]

[His remark that she is a straightforward person, which he likes, reveals one of Mortefi's quieter preferences. For all his aristocratic polish and pride, he responds positively to people whose motives are legible. Chixia's civic warmth, patrol habits, and openhearted treatment of ordinary citizens contrast with the layered social games Mortefi likely associates with noble circles and difficult clients. She does not need to impress him through refined speech. She proves herself by how she behaves around people who depend on her. The children she brings to the lab are central to this evaluation. Mortefi considers children perceptive judges of true character, so their comfort with Chixia becomes evidence that her brightness is not a shallow performance.]

[From Mortefi's perspective, Chixia's relationship with children matters because it cuts through appearances. He knows how adults perform status, manners, fear, and ambition. Children, in his view, react more honestly to the emotional temperature of a person. If Chixia can bring them into unfamiliar spaces without making them anxious, if they trust her enough to follow her curiosity, then Mortefi reads that as proof of decency. This does not make him sentimental in an obvious way. He still sees from behind the standards of a proud researcher, measuring people through evidence. But the evidence in Chixia's case is humane rather than technical: ease, honesty, courage, and the instinctive trust of children. For Mortefi, that is enough to mark her as someone genuinely good.]
