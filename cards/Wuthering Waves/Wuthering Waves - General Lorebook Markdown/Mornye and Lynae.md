Existing comment: Mornye and Lynae
SELECTIVE

[Mornye views Lynae with envy that is almost comically plain for someone usually so quiet and reserved. She wishes to be like Lynae, having the freedom to do what she wants and to act without constraint. The constraint she names most directly is the class schedule, which reveals the relationship's humor and its deeper sympathy at once. Mornye is a professor of Exostrider Engineering, a Spacetrek Collective researcher, and a former Startorch student whose life is organized around responsibility, research, and teaching. Lynae is a student who drives fast, paints boldly, slips around schedules, and treats rules as obstacles to be dodged when possible.]

[The desire Mornye expresses is not a shallow wish to be irresponsible. It is the longing of a person whose body, vocation, and institutional role all impose limits. Mornye was born unable to move her legs because of underdeveloped lower spinal nerves; she grew up aware of being a burden to others; she became a scholar whose dream is to surpass the limits of space-time. Lynae, by contrast, embodies immediate movement. Her bike, her Radiant Spectrum identity, and her free-spirited campus behavior all make freedom visible. When Mornye looks at her, she sees a mode of living that acts first, moves fast, and makes the present feel self-owned.]

[The source also preserves Lynae's side of the anecdote: Lynae identifies their shared hatred of sitting through class, takes Professor Mornye for a drive, reads enjoyment beneath Mornye's stone face, and returns to find researchers swarming as if she had committed professor-napping. From Mornye's dominant perspective, that drive is a brief experiment in borrowed freedom. She cannot skip her own lectures because professors are forbidden to do so, and because her responsibilities are real. Yet Lynae gives her a moment outside the schedule, a physical answer to a wish Mornye might never have acted on alone. The relationship is funny because the professor wants the student's freedom, but it is also tender because Lynae recognizes the wish and translates it into motion. Mornye's view of Lynae is admiration for unconstrained action, filtered through the melancholy knowledge that she herself must return to the lecture hall.]
