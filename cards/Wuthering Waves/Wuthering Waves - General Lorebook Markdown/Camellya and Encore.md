Existing comment: Camellya and Encore
SELECTIVE

[Camellya and Encore is framed by Camellya's predatory but playful view of potential. She identifies Encore first as the girl who loves stories, which shows that Camellya notices the bright imaginative surface before turning it into a future combat expectation. Encore belongs to the Black Shores' lively human side: energetic, affectionate, adventurous, story-loving, and often framed by her Wooly plush companions. To most observers, those qualities invite protectiveness or amusement. Camellya's response is different. She hopes Encore will mature into a strong opponent someday, because then they will have so much fun together. The wording is cheerful, but the underlying logic is Camellya's familiar Bloom Bearer instinct. She looks at people as seeds, possibilities, and future tests. Encore's present charm is not dismissed, but it is not the final point. The interesting question for Camellya is what strength might grow out of that imagination.]

[This perspective makes the relationship asymmetrical in a very Camellya-specific way. She does not view Encore primarily as a childlike companion, junior colleague, or Black Shores mascot. She views her as someone who may one day become entertaining in the highest form Camellya recognizes: a strong opponent. That does not mean Camellya wishes her harm in a simple cruel sense. Camellya's categories of affection, curiosity, and violence are tangled together by her pursuit of stimulation. A future duel can sound to her like friendship, celebration, and recognition all at once. Encore's love of stories may even make her more intriguing, because stories are a way of imagining danger before entering it, building heroic roles, monsters, companions, and endings inside the mind. Camellya, who follows fate and excitement with little patience for responsibility, can see that imaginative spark as something that might harden into real power later. Her dominant view stays focused on growth toward challenge. She respects what Encore could become more than what Encore currently represents to gentler people. In the Black Shores context, where consultants, guardians, candidates, and Bloom Bearers all orbit secrets and Lament threats, Camellya's hope is both affectionate and alarming: she wants Encore to grow strong enough to make play feel like battle, and battle feel like the best story they could share.]
