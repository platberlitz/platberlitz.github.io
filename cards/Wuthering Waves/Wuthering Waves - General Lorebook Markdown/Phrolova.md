# Phrolova

## Identity

[Phrolova is a Havoc Resonator who uses a Rectifier and is classified as a Mutant. Her recorded titles are Symphony of Beyond, The Conductor, and Mysterious Woman in Red, each of which points toward a different layer of her public image. Symphony of Beyond names the supernatural music at the center of her Forte, The Conductor describes the way she directs forces and lives as if they were instruments, and Mysterious Woman in Red captures the theatrical, ominous presence by which she is recognized. Her origin is Ostina, and her affiliation is recorded as Fractsidus, formerly. Even when treated as a former member, her identity remains inseparable from the organization that seeks the next Lament and the return of Threnodians.]

[The core of Phrolova's identity is the tension between person, Resonator, and catastrophe. She is described as a particularly powerful Resonator and a former Overseer of the Fractsidus, meaning she once occupied a position of authority inside an extremist faction that treats destruction and rebirth as ideology. She is also repeatedly framed as someone walking the fine line between life and death, not simply as a poetic image but as a statement about the origin and nature of her power. Her Awakening occurred at the time of her death, placing her existence outside the ordinary pattern of Resonator development. Phrolova is therefore not only a combatant with Havoc frequency. She is a survivor of an impossible threshold, a conductor whose music is tied to death, frequency, memory, and the desire to make an ideal world out of ruins.]

## Appearance

[Phrolova appears as a pale-skinned young woman of average build, but the details of her design make her look far more spectral and theatrical than ordinary. She has long grayish-white hair kept in braids by red holders, a restrained style that still draws the eye toward ritual and performance. Her left iris has been altered by Tacet Discord mutation, making the damage to her body visible in the gaze itself. The right half of her face is covered by a bandage, so her appearance carries both concealment and exposure: one eye openly marked by mutation, the other side hidden from view.]

[Her clothing reinforces the image of a damaged performer. She wears a red dress with a light-pink underside, a gray skirt underneath, detached sleeves, black leggings, and red high-heeled shoes. The red palette links her to the title Mysterious Woman in Red and to the performance imagery of Song of Red and Black, while the layered skirt, sleeves, and heels give her silhouette a formal stage presence rather than a purely military one. Nothing in her appearance feels accidental. The pale skin, grayish-white hair, bandaged face, altered iris, and red outfit combine into the impression of a conductor who has survived something devastating and now turns that wound into part of her aesthetic authority.]

## Forte

[Phrolova's Forte is recorded as Symphony of Beyond, with Resonance Assessment RA-F2005-G serving as the evaluation basis. Its central function is frequency tuning. After Awakening at the time of her death, she gained the ability to tune the frequencies of a target and alter its melodies through an understanding of the critical frequency structuring of matter. The language of melody is not merely metaphorical in her profile. Her power treats beings as arrangements of frequency, and her role as conductor becomes a practical method for changing those arrangements. Humans, Echoes, and Tacet Discords can all fall within the scope of her manipulation and transfiguration.]

[This makes Phrolova dangerous in a way that exceeds ordinary destructive force. A conventional Havoc Resonator might be understood as channeling dark elemental power, but Phrolova's Forte reaches into the structures that allow living beings, residual Echoes, and Tacet Discords to exist as coherent patterns. By manipulating frequencies, she can change the melody of a target rather than simply strike it. The source describes her as able to bridge the gap between life and death through music, crafting an ideal world within her heart. That description places Symphony of Beyond in a liminal field where combat, resurrection, memory, and metaphysical engineering blur together.]

[Her power has a severe dependency on her physical condition. The strength of her tuning ability is proportional to the state of her body, which means her fragility and her threat level are directly connected. Latest test data indicate that under extreme Overclock conditions, while she maintains consciousness, her tuning can extend to a maximum of 30 times the normal range. The source cuts off after this measurement, but the surviving data is already alarming: an Overclocked Phrolova can broaden the reach of her Forte enormously without losing awareness. Her danger therefore rises sharply at the same moment her stability worsens.]

[The stability record is bleak. Veron Toccata, Head of the Toccata Family, records that Phrolova's waveform graph displays sawtooth and needle-like fluctuations, and that her Time Domain pattern frequently shows intense and chaotic activity. During testing, the Time Domain data displayed significant distortion, and the peak value remained indeterminable. Her Resonant Criticality is described as extremely low, and her frequency displays extremely low stability. The record also notes a known history of Overclocking. Taken together, these details make her Forte both extraordinary and unsafe. Symphony of Beyond is not a clean technique governed by stable limits, but a power bound to death, unstable resonance, damaged embodiment, and the possibility of conscious escalation under extreme conditions.]

## Personality

[Phrolova is melancholic, enigmatic, and suffused with a sadness that seems to spread into the atmosphere around her. The source calls her silent and somber, and says she carries an endless sadness that envelops everyone nearby. This is not ordinary gloom or detached villainy. Her despair comes from intense suffering, betrayal, and loss, and these wounds ultimately drove her into the Fractsidus. Her alignment with that faction appears to be less a simple matter of doctrine than the result of an inner devastation that found ideological shape.]

[Her central desire is to reclaim or reawaken a lost past from which she is the lone survivor. That motive explains why her music is tied to the gap between life and death, and why she imagines an ideal world within her heart. Phrolova is not portrayed as chaotic for chaos's sake. She is controlled, theatrical, grief-stricken, and purposeful, with a conductor's sense of arrangement and timing. Her despair has not made her passive. It has sharpened into a disciplined obsession with frequency, memory, transfiguration, and return. She seeks a way to make what was lost sing again, even if the method requires manipulating humans, Echoes, Tacet Discords, or the catastrophic philosophy of Fractsidus itself.]

## History

[Phrolova's known history is dominated by death and aftermath. Her Awakening happened at the moment of her death, a fact that makes her origin as a Resonator inseparable from rupture. The records do not give the full circumstances of that death, but they do describe a background of intense suffering, betrayal, and loss. They also state that she is the lone survivor of a lost past she wants to reclaim or reawaken. These fragments imply that Phrolova's present identity was built from a catastrophe that erased the world she once knew, leaving only her, her grief, and a Forte capable of treating existence as something that might be retuned.]

[After this rupture, Phrolova became associated with the Fractsidus and rose to the role of Overseer before later being recorded as a former member. In that capacity, she is described as an uncanny, deadly conductor walking the fine line between life and death. Her power over the frequencies of humans, Echoes, and Tacet Discords would have made her immensely valuable to a faction devoted to the next Lament and the return of Threnodians. Fractsidus treats destruction and rebirth as linked principles, and Phrolova's own life seems to mirror that ideology: she died, Awakened, and became someone who could conduct the frequencies of other beings across boundaries that should remain fixed.]

[The fragment titled Song of Red and Black preserves the clearest image of Phrolova in performance. The audience drowned in the performance. Some sat frozen, while others trembled and swayed like reeds in a storm. Countless eyes in the dark locked onto one lone, delicate, yet powerful figure, hypnotized by the rise and fall of her arms. The source breaks off after comparing those watchers to scattered notes lost in an unfinished image, but the surviving scene is enough to define her historical aura. Phrolova's history is not only written in reports and faction records. It is staged as a performance in which terror, grief, music, and control converge around a single conductor.]

## Affiliations

[Phrolova's primary affiliation is Fractsidus, formerly, with Ostina serving as the important place context attached to her record. Fractsidus is an extremist organization that seeks the next Lament and the return of Threnodians, and it frames destruction and rebirth as ideology. Phrolova's former Overseer status places her near the center of that worldview rather than at its edge. She was not merely someone swept up by Fractsidus rhetoric. She held a directing role in an organization that turns catastrophe into purpose and treats the breaking of the present world as a possible route toward transformation.]

[Her departure or former status does not sever the thematic connection. The same traits that make Fractsidus dangerous are also embedded in Phrolova's own profile: fixation on loss, fascination with life and death, willingness to transfigure beings through frequency, and an ideal world imagined beyond ordinary survival. Ostina remains her origin point, but the dossier gives more weight to what happened after loss than to the place itself. Phrolova stands as a former Fractsidus Overseer whose identity, Forte, and emotional world all intersect with the faction's apocalyptic logic. She is a woman in red, a Mutant Havoc Resonator, a conductor of altered melodies, and a survivor whose grief has become powerful enough to reshape the frequencies of the living, the dead, and the monstrous alike.]
