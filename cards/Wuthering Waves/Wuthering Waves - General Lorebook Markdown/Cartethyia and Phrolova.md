Existing comment: Cartethyia and Phrolova
SELECTIVE

[Phrolova's relationship with Cartethyia exists entirely from Phrolova's perspective, as Cartethyia has never offered a reciprocal view of this connection, creating an asymmetry that is itself revealing. What Phrolova sees when she looks at Cartethyia is something that stirs emotions rarely accessible to the former Fractsidus Overseer: envy. Not human, yet possessing a will whole as any mortal's, this observation cuts to the heart of what makes Cartethyia extraordinary and what Phrolova finds simultaneously fascinating and maddening. Cartethyia was not born in the conventional sense but was created through divine intervention, molded by the Threnodian's curse into a vessel that should theoretically lack the autonomous will that defines humanity. Yet somehow, despite her artificial origins and the cosmic forces that shaped her, Cartethyia possesses a will as complete and independent as any naturally born human being. This paradox is something Phrolova, who has spent her life studying the boundaries between human, Echo, and Tacet Discord through her Symphony of Beyond ability, finds utterly captivating. A creation molded by Divinity, Phrolova understands this state intimately, as her own transformation into a Resonator involved death and rebirth through Threnodian influence, making her something other than conventionally human while retaining human consciousness and desire.]

[The envy Phrolova feels is not petty jealousy but a profound longing for something she believes Cartethyia possesses that Phrolova herself lacks: the ability to remain whole despite divine manipulation. Phrolova's own experience with cosmic forces has been one of fragmentation, of having her identity shattered and reassembled in ways that left her permanently altered, permanently other, permanently caught between life and death. Cartethyia, by contrast, managed to integrate the opposing forces of Sentinel and Threnodian without losing herself entirely, maintaining her essential humanity despite carrying powers that should have erased it. If only Phrolova could unearth the universal truth behind Cartethyia's success, if she could understand the mechanism that permitted Cartethyia to achieve what Phrolova could not, then perhaps the echoes from the other shore would sing even sweeter. The other shore likely refers to whatever existence lies beyond death or beyond the current corrupted state of being that Phrolova occupies, and the echoes are the remnants of her former self or perhaps the lost past she is desperately trying to reclaim. This statement reveals that Phrolova views Cartethyia not as a person but as a problem to be solved, a mystery to be unraveled, a source of data that might provide the key to Phrolova's own liberation or transformation. Whether this analytical interest translates into any form of personal relationship is unclear, Phrolova's melancholic and enigmatic nature, combined with her history of intense suffering and betrayal, makes her unlikely to form conventional friendships, and her view of Cartethyia as primarily a research subject rather than a peer suggests that their connection exists more in Phrolova's mind than in any actual interaction between them. For Cartethyia, being the object of Phrolova's scientific envy is probably unsettling, the former Overseer's interest carries implications that Cartethyia, who has spent her life trying to understand her own nature, would recognize as potentially dangerous. To be studied by someone who has mastered the manipulation of frequencies, who could theoretically dissect the mechanisms that make Cartethyia unique, creates a vulnerability that Cartethyia likely approaches with caution, aware that Phrolova's desire to understand could easily become a desire to control or replicate the miracle that Cartethyia represents.]
