# Xiangli Yao and Baizhi

## Directional Relationship

[Xiangli Yao views Baizhi first as a serious researcher, not as a spectacle, subordinate, or casual colleague. Their work does not often overlap directly, which matters because his respect is not built on constant personal contact or institutional familiarity alone. He has read her reports and judges them to be well informed and persuasive. For Xiangli Yao, that is a meaningful standard. As Huaxu Academy's Principal Investigator in charge of academics at the Jinzhou Campus, he evaluates work through evidence, structure, and the ability to turn observation into knowledge others can use. Baizhi meets that standard through the quality of her records.]

[Baizhi's reserved manner and reliance on observation, data, and study make her legible to Xiangli Yao in professional terms. He is himself a gentle yet rational scientist whose own body, prosthetic arm, and Matter Weaver Forte are tied to unresolved technical questions. He understands the value of caution around incomplete evidence. Baizhi's reputation for careful judgment would appeal to him because it protects research from exaggeration. She does not need to perform brilliance loudly to be taken seriously by him. Her reports persuade because they carry the discipline of fieldwork, and Xiangli Yao is especially attuned to that kind of credibility.]

[The relationship also highlights two different modes of Academy-adjacent science. Xiangli Yao's profile is institutional, technological, and academic: he stands at the center of Huaxu Academy's Jinzhou Campus, associated with Automata Mechanics, prosthetic-linked Resonance, and formal responsibility. Baizhi's value often appears through field research, returning with data that peers consider valuable. From Xiangli Yao's perspective, this makes her work complementary even when it is not directly shared. The Academy depends not only on those who design systems, but also on those who enter uncertain environments and bring back observations strong enough to support later analysis.]

[Xiangli Yao's regard for Baizhi is therefore quiet, professional, and precise. He does not frame her as an assistant to his own genius or as a mystery to be solved. He identifies the concrete merits of her work: informed analysis, persuasive reporting, and repeated contributions from the field. If he relies on Aalto for rare materials and human networks, Baizhi represents another kind of reliability: disciplined empirical knowledge. In Xiangli Yao's eyes, she strengthens the scientific world they share by proving that careful research can travel between the field and the page without losing rigor.]
