Existing comment: Cartethyia and Carlotta
SELECTIVE

[Cartethyia's relationship with Carlotta Montelli is characterized by genuine admiration and a surprising sense of kinship between two women from very different worlds who nonetheless share a commitment to Ragunna's future. As the daughter of the Montelli family, one of Ragunna's most prominent and influential houses, Carlotta has brought fresh ideas to the city, challenging traditional ways of thinking and pushing for innovation in areas where stagnation has been the norm. Cartethyia recognizes the importance of this contribution, understanding that if Rinascita's island were to remain isolated from outside influence, the air would grow stifling both literally and metaphorically. The people of Ragunna need new ships to expand their reach, new horizons to explore beyond the familiar waters they have sailed for generations, and new discoveries to challenge their assumptions about what is possible. Carlotta represents exactly this spirit of expansion and renewal, using her position within the Montelli family to advocate for change rather than simply maintaining the status quo that her noble birth would allow her to comfortably preserve. Cartethyia's appreciation for this quality reveals her own desire for Ragunna's growth and development, her understanding that the island's future depends not on clinging to ancient traditions but on adapting to new circumstances while preserving what is genuinely valuable from the past.]

[Despite her sacred office and cosmic power, Cartethyia freely admits her lack of skill in business matters, displaying a humility that is rare among those who hold significant authority. She recalls that her nanny in the Order always said she did not even know how to haggle, a charming anecdote that humanizes her in a way that discussions of her divine mandate never could. This admission of practical incompetence in mundane affairs creates an interesting contrast with Carlotta's evident business acumen, establishing a dynamic where Cartethyia is the one who needs to learn rather than the one who teaches. Her expressed desire to learn a few tricks from Miss Carlotta if she ever gets the chance is not merely polite flattery but genuine interest in acquiring skills that her role as Blessed Maiden never required her to develop. This relationship reveals a side of Cartethyia that rarely surfaces in other contexts: her curiosity about ordinary life, her awareness of her own limitations outside the realm of cosmic conflict, and her willingness to seek mentorship from someone whose expertise lies in practical matters rather than divine warfare. For Carlotta, Cartethyia's respect likely carries significant weight, the Blessed Maiden's endorsement of her progressive vision validates Carlotta's efforts to modernize Ragunna's economy and society, providing a form of spiritual authority that can counterbalance the conservative forces who resist change within the Montelli family and the broader aristocracy. Their relationship is thus mutually beneficial: Cartethyia gains insight into the practical workings of commerce and governance that her sacred education never covered, while Carlotta receives the implicit support of one of Rinascita's most important spiritual figures, strengthening her position in the internal politics that determine whether her innovations succeed or fail.]
