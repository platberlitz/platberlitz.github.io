# Camellya

## Identity

[Camellya is an Artificial-class Resonator aligned with the Havoc frequency, a Sword user, and the bearer of the title Sanguine Blossom. Her formal affiliation and national context are both tied to the Black Shores, while her personal origin is listed as unknown. Within Black Shores records she is identified as a Bloom Bearer, a role connected to the combat-oriented germination tests administered to new candidates. That position gives her a clear institutional function, but Camellya herself does not frame it as a solemn duty. She refuses the usual weight of responsibility and treats the work as a convenient channel for pleasure, challenge, and pursuit. Her identity is therefore split between official classification and personal appetite: on paper she is a Black Shores Resonator who tests candidates, but in practice she is a restless combatant who follows whatever promise of excitement catches her attention. The phrase she uses for interesting people, "promising seeds," suits her title and imagery, turning strength, potential, danger, and growth into the same obsession.]

## Appearance

[Camellya appears as a short young woman with white hair streaked with black and worn in twintails. Her eyes are dark gray, sharpened by red diamond-shaped pupils that make her gaze feel less innocent than her silhouette might first suggest. She wears a short ruffled white dress accented in red, gold, and black, with asymmetrical white stockings and black Blake Bloom flower motifs worked through the outfit. The resulting image is delicate at first glance, almost decorative, but the details steadily change the impression. The floral language does not soften her so much as warn the viewer about what kind of beauty she represents. Flower and vine elements echo her Sanguine Blossom title, while the Blake Bloom motifs connect her to the Black Shores and their symbolic vocabulary. Her design repeatedly joins ornament and threat: pale fabric with crimson accents, girlish twintails with predatory eyes, cultivated blossoms with dangerous corruption. The source describes these flower-and-vine details as reflecting not only delicacy, but also the corruption and obsession tied to her Resonance.]

## Forte

[Camellya's associated Forte is Sanguine Blossom, recorded in a partially redacted Resonance Assessment numbered 2 redacted G. The exact timing of her Awakening is unclear because the record cites a redacted disorder, but it confirms that she has been able to use her Forte since that Awakening. Her Tacet Mark is located on her chest. After Awakening, her body began showing clear signs of vegetative somatization, which makes the floral theme more than costume or metaphor. Her Resonance has altered her body, especially her right arm and head, and the severity of those changes intensifies alongside frequency fluctuations. The preserved assessment states that her Resonance Spectrum Pattern closely resembles something, but the available entry cuts off before the comparison is complete. Even with that gap, the medical and visual evidence consistently tie her power to growth, bloom, physical alteration, and hazardous resonance behavior.]

[Her stability data is more alarming than her casual manner suggests. Resonator Camellya's waveform graph displays needle-like fluctuations, and her Time Domain pattern shows intense, chaotic activity at an extremely high frequency. During testing, the Time Domain data was significantly distorted, leaving the peak value indeterminable. Her Resonant Criticality is listed as low, yet her frequency stability is also extremely low, creating a high risk of Overclocking. She has a known history of Overclocking, although the record preserves the maximum recorded rating only as an unfinished label. The important conclusion is still clear: Camellya is powerful, unstable, and physically altered by her Forte in ways that are not merely cosmetic. Her danger is not limited to what she chooses to do in battle. It is also present in what her own Resonance does to her, how violently her frequency behaves, and how little certainty the examiners can retain when her data begins to distort.]

## Personality

[At her core, Camellya is driven by amusement, stimulation, and the thrill of measuring herself against others. Compared with other Black Shores members, she treats employment as an obligation and little more. Her source profile calls her a hedonist, and that word is precise: she pursues pleasure through conflict, curiosity, and the discovery of people strong enough to entertain her. She is especially delighted by promising seeds, but her interest is not restricted to official recruits or to people selected by the Tethys System as candidates. The same appetite can extend toward civilians and criminals if they appear interesting enough. This makes her unpredictable in a way that differs from ordinary malice. Camellya is not portrayed as someone who needs a grand ideology before acting. She needs a spark, a possibility, a sign that someone might become fun to chase, test, or fight.]

[Her personality is laid-back on the surface, but the source explicitly pairs that ease with an aroma of danger. She is free from shackles in both conduct and attitude, intrigued by fate and willing to wander according to vague guidance or a chain of scenarios that catches her imagination. Responsibility bores her; risk does not deter her; the promise of a good fight can override caution. The identity entry is visibly truncated while describing how she abandons risks in pursuit of battle, yet the surviving wording makes the point unmistakable. Camellya is a person whose sense of play can become perilous because she does not sharply separate entertainment from harm. Her flower imagery captures that contradiction well. She can appear decorative, teasing, almost whimsical, but the blossom is sanguine, rooted in violence and resonance instability.]

## History

[Camellya's known history is fragmentary, and that incompleteness is part of how the records define her. Her origin is unknown, the timing of her Awakening is unclear, and a later historical note titled To the Days Ahead is preserved only as an unfinished beginning. What can be established is that she awakened as a Havoc Resonator, has been capable of using Sanguine Blossom since that point, and subsequently displayed vegetative somatization severe enough to affect her right arm and head. Her body and identity became entangled with the bloom imagery that now surrounds her. At some point she entered the Black Shores and became a Bloom Bearer, serving in a capacity that places her before new candidates and tests their combat potential. The repeated emphasis on germination tests suggests that her history inside the organization is not passive membership. She is part of the process by which potential is discovered, stressed, and forced to reveal whether it can survive.]

[The available history also presents Camellya as someone who wanders by choice. She is intrigued by the belief in fate and moves across the lands according to her own will, following an uncertain chain of events rather than a stable itinerary. This wandering quality helps explain why her official post never fully contains her. She may belong to the Black Shores, but the record describes her as free, dangerous, and personally motivated by battle. Her known Overclocking history adds another layer of risk to that biography. Camellya's past is not simply the story of a recruit becoming an operative; it is the story of an unstable Resonator whose power changed her body, whose organization found a use for her appetite, and whose personal sense of fate keeps pulling her toward new encounters.]

## Affiliations

[Camellya belongs to the Black Shores, with the important place context recorded as unknown and The Black Shores. Her role as Bloom Bearer makes her more than a loosely associated fighter. She participates in the organization's candidate-facing machinery, especially the combat-oriented germination tests that identify and pressure potential members. The Tethys System appears in her profile as one source of candidates, but Camellya's interest extends beyond that official scope, which means her affiliation does not fully regulate her behavior. She stands inside the Black Shores while also resisting the discipline one might expect from such a position. That tension is central to her usefulness and her danger. The organization gains a formidable tester, a Havoc Resonator, and a living symbol of Bloom Bearer peril; it also has to accommodate someone who sees the role as an obligation and seeks excitement wherever she can find it. Camellya is therefore best understood as a Black Shores asset who embodies the group's bloom symbolism in its most volatile form: beautiful, cultivated, artificial, unstable, and eager to draw blood when a promising seed appears.]
