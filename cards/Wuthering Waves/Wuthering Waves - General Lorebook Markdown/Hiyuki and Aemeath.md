Existing comment: Hiyuki and Aemeath
SELECTIVE

[Hiyuki views Aemeath through the lens of a miko who has seen endurance become both virtue and wound. Her first judgment is not tactical, institutional, or scientific: Aemeath is a resilient child. That phrasing matters because Hiyuki herself is an old bearer of duty, bound to Ashinohara's vanished traditions, Suzu, the Special Response Force, and Lahai-Roi's abnormal crises. She does not call Aemeath a case file, a Synchronist, a digital ghost, or a failed Exostrider incident. She begins with the human fact that a young person endured hardship severe enough that returning at all feels almost impossible. Hiyuki's compassion is restrained, but the restraint does not diminish it. It makes the recognition sharper, because Hiyuki is not easily moved by ordinary reports of suffering.]

[Her perspective is also shaped by absence. When Aemeath vanished within the simulator cockpit, Hiyuki was away on a field assignment, fulfilling another duty while a catastrophe unfolded at Startorch Academy. The investigation team later briefed her, so her knowledge arrives through official reconstruction rather than direct witness. Hiyuki is accustomed to such delayed understanding: duty often denies people the right to be present at the moment their care would have mattered most. That delay leaves a quiet pressure in her view of Aemeath. She cannot undo the disappearance, cannot restore the body lost through overclocking, and cannot fully know what Aemeath felt when she reached for Exostrider. She can only weigh the report and honor the girl behind it.]

[The detail that makes Hiyuki pause is Aemeath's aspiration to save the world. To someone like Hiyuki, such a phrase is never merely childish. She knows that wishes entrusted in passing can become lifelong burdens, and that a vow to save others can demand a terrible tithe from the self. Her wonder over Aemeath's upbringing is therefore cautious and reverent: what warmth, pain, teaching, or loneliness could make a student believe so fiercely in world-saving that she would press beyond safe limits? Hiyuki does not answer that question for her. She preserves it as an unresolved mystery, seeing in Aemeath a bright conviction that survived even after the body did not.]
