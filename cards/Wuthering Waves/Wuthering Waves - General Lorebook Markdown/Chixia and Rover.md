## Directional Relationship

[Directional POV: Chixia views Rover. Chixia describes Rover as someone who does not talk much, but is very strong in battle. Her comparison comes through a Huanglong saying: "A cat that catches mice doesn't meow." This is a perfectly Chixia way to understand a quiet hero. She does not need elaborate explanations, cryptic destiny, or a complete account of Rover's lost memories. She sees the practical evidence: Rover acts, fights, protects, and proves themselves through results rather than noise.]

[Chixia's perspective is shaped by patrol work. As a junior Patroller in Jinzhou's Public Security Bureau, she is trained by daily life to value people who show up when needed. Talk can reassure citizens, and Chixia herself uses cheer as part of her service, but she also understands that words are not the only measure of reliability. Rover's quiet strength fits the saying because competence does not have to announce itself. A cat that catches mice has already done the work. In the same way, Rover's silence becomes less awkward to Chixia once battle has shown what kind of person stands behind it.]

[The proverb also reveals how Chixia translates extraordinary people into familiar local wisdom. Rover is an amnesiac wanderer whose role reaches far beyond one city's alleys, but Chixia makes them understandable through Huanglong common sense. That translation is affectionate rather than reductive. It lets her place Rover within her social world, where practical sayings, food, patrol routes, and neighborhood warmth help people process strange events. Rover may be mysterious to many, but Chixia can accept mystery if the person reliably protects others. Her trust begins from action, not explanation.]

[This relationship should remain Chixia's directional view of Rover. It is not a general protagonist dossier, nor a mutual account of companions. Rover appears through Chixia's bright, grounded judgment: quiet, strong, and useful in the way a good hunter is useful without boasting. Her admiration has humor in it, but also respect. She recognizes that the world needs many kinds of protectors, including those who do not fill the air with words. For Chixia, Rover's silence is not emptiness. It is the quiet before effective action.]
