Existing comment: Lynae and Fractsidus
SELECTIVE

[Lynae views Fractsidus with blunt rejection, and that bluntness should not be softened into abstract political disagreement. To her, Fractsidus is an organization full of crazies, a harsh judgment she immediately refuses to walk back because lived danger has given her no reason to be polite. Her connection to Fractsidus is not ideological sympathy or former loyalty. It comes through the stolen identity that allowed her to escape the Lawless Zone and enter Startorch Academy. The real Lynae was a Fractsidus operative attending the Academy as a spy, and the current Lynae took that name to get out of one violent world by hiding inside the cover built by another.]

[That history makes Fractsidus both personal and threatening from Lynae's perspective. The organization is not just a distant extremist faction trying to bring about the True Lament through catastrophic frequency events, Threnodian worship, and destabilizing operations. It is the shadow inside her student file, the origin of the name she wears, and the force most likely to punish her if the truth of the impersonation becomes known. Lynae wants a campus life that can remain trouble-free forever, but Fractsidus represents exactly the kind of old violence that could collapse that hope. Even when she jokes, drives fast, skips class, or tries to act like ordinary student consequences are her biggest problem, this danger sits beneath the surface.]

[Her statement that she is done with them reveals a boundary more than a completed fact. Lynae may want no further dealings with Fractsidus, but the stolen cover means the organization may not be done with her. If Startorch Academy discovers the truth, she risks institutional consequences; if Fractsidus discovers it, she risks becoming a loose end in a spy operation whose original asset has been replaced. That double exposure explains why her revulsion is so sharp. Fractsidus took catastrophe and made it doctrine. Lynae took one of its lies and tried to convert it into a normal future. From her perspective, there is no romance in their apocalypse, no dignity in their schemes, and no reason to grant them nuance in casual speech. They are the danger she escaped around, the conspiracy she stole from, and the problem she sincerely hopes never again comes roaring back into her life.]
