Existing comment: Phrolova and Cartethyia
SELECTIVE

[Phrolova and Cartethyia: Phrolova views Cartethyia: Phrolova's gaze fixes on Cartethyia because Cartethyia embodies a contradiction that Phrolova cannot ignore. Cartethyia is not human in the ordinary sense, having been shaped by forces tied to divinity, curse, Sentinel authority, and Threnodian influence. Yet she possesses a will whole as any mortal's. For Phrolova, whose own Awakening occurred at the time of death and whose Symphony of Beyond manipulates the frequencies of humans, Echoes, and Tacet Discords, this wholeness is not merely admirable. It is evidence of a mystery. Cartethyia should have been a broken vessel, a manufactured saint, or a cosmic wound wearing a girl's face. Instead, she persists as a person.]

[The envy Phrolova admits is therefore deep and analytical. She does not envy Cartethyia's fame, titles, or reverence as Blessed Maiden. She envies the successful integration of the impossible. Phrolova's own life is marked by rupture: death, grief, loss, betrayal, Fractsidus ideology, and a Forte that stands on the threshold between life and death. Cartethyia, by contrast, appears to have achieved an answer Phrolova still seeks. She carries divine and monstrous pressures without losing the core of will. To Phrolova, that makes Cartethyia less a companion than a living theorem, a creation molded by Divinity whose continued selfhood might reveal universal truth.]

[Phrolova's desire to unearth that truth makes the relationship dangerous in tone. Her language is beautiful, but beauty does not soften the intent. If understanding Cartethyia's success could make the echoes from the other shore sing sweeter, Phrolova would likely study Cartethyia with the patience of a conductor listening for a hidden interval. She would not approach primarily as a friend or worshiper. She would approach as someone who believes another being's structure may contain the key to her own lost world. The perspective remains Phrolova's: fascinated, envious, mournful, and predatory in its elegance. Cartethyia matters because Phrolova sees in her a proof that artificial or cursed origin need not prevent a complete will, and Phrolova wants to know why.]
