## Directional Relationship

[Directional POV: Changli views Sanhua. Changli sees Sanhua as composed, silent, vigilant, and constantly alert in her role as Jinhsi's bodyguard. This perspective is practical and affectionate at once. Changli understands the necessity of such vigilance because Jinhsi is not only a young Magistrate, but the appointed Resonator of Sentinel Jue and the symbolic heart of Jinzhou's future. A bodyguard near her cannot afford casual attention. Sanhua's silence and discipline therefore earn Changli's respect, since they show a person who has turned protection into a continuous state of being.]

[Yet Changli's view does not stop at professional approval. She says that both Jinhsi and she have always wished Sanhua would take a well-deserved rest and let others shoulder part of the burden. This reveals the emotional center of the relationship. Changli can admire Sanhua's devotion while also recognizing the danger of devotion that never relaxes. As a counselor, strategist, and teacher, Changli is trained to notice unsustainable patterns. Sanhua's constant readiness protects Jinhsi, but it also risks making Sanhua disappear into function, as if the bodyguard's only permitted identity were alertness.]

[Changli's wish for Sanhua to rest parallels her concern for Jinhsi's overwork. Around the Magistrate, Changli sees multiple people who define themselves through duty to the point of self-erasure. Jinhsi gives herself to Jinzhou. Sanhua gives herself to guarding Jinhsi. Changli's warmth expresses itself by wanting both of them to accept support. She does not ask Sanhua to become less loyal. Instead, she wants Sanhua to trust that loyalty can include receiving help, sharing strain, and surviving long enough to continue protecting what matters. That is a strategist's compassion: not sentimental indulgence, but maintenance of the people who hold the city together.]

[This entry should keep Changli's view dominant. Sanhua is not profiled symmetrically as a Glacio sword user or bodyguard in full; she is Sanhua as Changli observes her from within City Hall's circle: silent, composed, indispensable, and overburdened. Changli's respect is inseparable from concern. She sees the beauty of Sanhua's vigilance, but also the cost hidden inside it. Her hope that Sanhua will rest is a quiet act of care from someone who knows that even the strongest protectors must be protected from the habit of carrying everything alone.]
