Existing comment: Jinhsi and Changli
SELECTIVE

[Jinhsi views Changli as her esteemed teacher, and that word sets the relationship's direction. Changli is more than Jinzhou's Counselor, more than a former Secretary-General from Mingting, and more than a Fusion Resonator with a warm smile and a strategic mind. To Jinhsi, she is the person who has taught her much over the years, shaping the young Magistrate's ability to think, rule, listen, and endure. Jinhsi's own life has been defined by public responsibility from birth: Sentinel Jué's appointed Resonator, the young guardian of Jinzhou, and the civic figure expected to lead a frontier city toward tomorrow. Changli's teaching gives that responsibility practical form.]

[The admiration is sincere, but it is not naive. Jinhsi notices that Changli's smiling eyes still hold mysteries she cannot yet comprehend. That uncertainty does not weaken trust. It deepens it. Changli's warmth has always been paired with calculation, patience, and layers of unspoken memory. Her childhood fire, her experience in the capital's central administration, and her quiet influence inside City Hall all make her a woman who reveals only what the moment requires. Jinhsi, who is kindhearted and willing to ask for help, understands enough to know that her teacher has not made herself fully transparent.]

[From Jinhsi's perspective, that mystery is part of the lesson. A ruler cannot demand total simplicity from those who guide her, especially when governance itself is made of hidden pressures, delayed consequences, and people whose motives rarely show all at once. Changli teaches through counsel, example, restraint, and silence. Her smile can reassure the city, conceal pain, and hold a strategy in place until the right time. Jinhsi remains the student in this directional relationship, not because she lacks authority, but because she recognizes that authority must keep learning. Her reverence for Changli is therefore mature: gratitude for what has been taught, respect for what remains veiled, and patience for the day when she may understand the full depth behind those smiling eyes.]
