Existing comment: Hiyuki and Lucilla
SELECTIVE

[Hiyuki's view of Lucilla is built on gratitude she does not loudly display. Lucilla insists their partnership is only contractual, a practical arrangement between adults within Lahai-Roi's institutional world. Hiyuki accepts the wording, but she is not fooled by it. She knows that without Lucilla's discreet arrangements, she would have no place in the Spacetrek Collective. For a figure like Hiyuki, who belongs to Ashinohara's vanished sacred tradition, the Special Response Force, Startorch Academy, and classified Spacetrek structures all at once, having a place is not a minor convenience. It is the difference between being useful as a controlled participant and being left outside systems that still need her knowledge, power, and presence.]

[Lucilla's method matters to Hiyuki because it is quiet. As President of Startorch Academy, Lucilla is an idealist with practical feet on the ground, a guardian of students and institutional futures rather than a person who performs benevolence for applause. Hiyuki recognizes that kind of care because it resembles duty stripped of ornament. Lucilla can frame assistance as contract, policy, or necessary coordination, yet the effect is still protection. She makes space for Hiyuki without forcing Hiyuki to receive it as charity. That distinction is important. A century-burdened miko does not easily accept pity, and Lucilla's discretion allows dignity to remain intact.]

[From Hiyuki's perspective, the relationship is therefore neither sentimental friendship nor cold employment. It is a compact sustained by mutual awareness. Lucilla understands that Hiyuki's sacred history, Suzu-linked possibilities, and connection to Lahai-Roi's crises cannot be processed like an ordinary staff appointment. Hiyuki understands that Lucilla's insistence on contractual language protects both of them from unnecessary exposure. The gratitude remains controlled, but it is real. Hiyuki sees the President not as a benefactor demanding thanks, but as a savvy guardian who knows how to build a place for someone difficult to categorize. In a life shaped by vanished homelands and classified duties, that quiet place is no small gift.]
