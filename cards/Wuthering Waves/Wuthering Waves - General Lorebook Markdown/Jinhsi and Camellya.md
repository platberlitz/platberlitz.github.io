Existing comment: Jinhsi and Camellya
SELECTIVE

[Although this file is keyed as Jinhsi and Camellya, the preserved directional POV belongs to Camellya viewing Jinhsi. Camellya looks at the Loong Maiden of Jinzhou and immediately thinks of the Shorekeeper: two figures devoted to their duties, heavy with self-sacrifice, and seemingly willing to let obligation consume the self. To many citizens of Jinzhou, Jinhsi's devotion is reassuring. She is the young Magistrate, Sentinel Jué's appointed Resonator, and a symbol of Thawborn Renewal whose Spectro power and civic office promise the city's future. To Camellya, that same devotion is baffling. She sees the burden before she sees the honor.]

[Camellya's question, why burden themselves so much, reveals her own value system as much as it judges Jinhsi. Camellya is a Black Shores Bloom Bearer, a Havoc Resonator driven by amusement, battle, promising seeds, and the pursuit of stimulation. Responsibility bores her when it behaves like a shackle. Jinhsi, by contrast, lives inside chosen and inherited constraint: City Hall, Huanglong, Jinzhou, the Sentinel bond, public hope, and the promise to defend her people to her last breath. Camellya can recognize strength and beauty in such a person, but she struggles to understand why anyone would submit to that much public need when freedom, danger, and desire exist elsewhere.]

[Her comparison to Shorekeeper adds a sharper edge. Camellya has seen duty become identity in the Black Shores, and she resists that pattern with her own unstable, wandering appetite. Jinhsi unsettles her because she appears to choose a similar weight without obvious resentment. Camellya does not turn Jinhsi into a full civic biography. Through her eyes, Jinhsi becomes a luminous contradiction: powerful enough to stand beside a Sentinel, gentle enough to care for a city, and self-denying enough to seem almost incomprehensible. The relationship remains Camellya's directional reading of Jinhsi as a devoted figure whose greatest mystery is not strength, but willingness to carry so much.]
