## Definition

[A Tacet Mark is the visible, physical sign that a person has undergone Resonator Awakening. It appears as a distinctive bodily mark or pattern, unique to each individual, and serves as the primary external indicator that someone possesses resonant abilities. The Tacet Mark is not merely cosmetic; it is a living inscription of the Resonator's frequency signature, permanently etched into their flesh upon Awakening.]

## Appearance and Location

[The location of a Tacet Mark differs by individual and bears no predictable correlation to the Resonator's Forte or element. Some bear their marks on visible areas such as the face, neck, hands, or forearms, while others carry them hidden beneath clothing on the torso, back, or thighs. The patterns themselves range from geometric lines and angular fractures to organic, flowing designs resembling veins, waves, or calligraphy. The coloration often reflects the Resonator's elemental affinity, with Glacio Resonators sometimes displaying pale blue tracery and Havoc Resonators showing marks of deep violet or black.]

## Significance and Identification

[In post-Lament society, Tacet Marks serve as an immediate identifier. In many city-states, showing one's Tacet Mark is part of official registration processes, and Resonator organizations maintain archives cataloguing mark patterns alongside Rabelle's Curve data and Resonance Spectrum Patterns. The mark can also shift subtly in response to the Resonator's state; during Overclocking, marks have been observed to glow, crack, or spread beyond their normal boundaries, a deeply alarming sign to anyone trained to recognize the symptoms.]

## Cultural Weight

[Tacet Marks carry significant cultural weight. In some regions, they are seen as symbols of strength and protection. In others, they provoke fear or suspicion, marking the bearer as someone whose power could turn catastrophic. Resonators in positions of authority, such as Augusta of Septimont whose mark is part of her public identity as Ephor, may display their marks proudly as badges of office. Others conceal them to avoid discrimination. The mark itself cannot be removed or hidden through ordinary means, making it an inescapable part of a Resonator's identity.]
