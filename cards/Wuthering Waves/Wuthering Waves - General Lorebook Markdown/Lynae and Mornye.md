Existing comment: Lynae and Mornye
SELECTIVE

[Lynae views Mornye through an unexpected point of kinship: both of them hate sitting through class. That is a funny claim because one of them is a Preparatory Program student with a known taste for freedom, speed, and schedule trouble, while the other is Professor Mornye, a Spacetrek Collective researcher and Startorch Academy professor of Exostrider Engineering. From Lynae's perspective, the difference in status only makes the shared impulse more entertaining. Mornye may be a brilliant academic with a dream of surpassing space-time, but Lynae senses the same restlessness under the formal title. A professor can be trapped by a lecture schedule as surely as a student can be trapped by attendance rules.]

[The drive Lynae gives Mornye is the center of the relationship from Lynae's side. Lynae's bike represents movement, risk, escape, and vivid present-tense life. Taking Mornye for a ride is therefore not just a prank or a joyride. It is Lynae offering her own language of freedom to someone whose body, work, and professorial duties all impose constraints. Mornye keeps a total stone face the whole time, which fits her quiet, not very talkative personality, but Lynae insists she could tell Mornye was loving it. That insistence reveals Lynae's confidence in reading suppressed pleasure. She may not speak in academic terms, but she understands when someone rigidly composed is secretly alive to the thrill.]

[The aftermath completes the comedy and the emotional point. When they return, a whole pack of Mornye's researchers swarms the gates as if Lynae has committed professor-napping. Lynae's reaction, that she likes her life here and is not trying to ruin it, keeps her recklessness bounded by self-preservation. She wants trouble-free campus life too much to truly endanger it, even when she bends rules for fun. In Mornye, she finds an adult who does not fit the stiff authority stereotype. Mornye's own source perspective confirms the connection: she wishes to be like Lynae, to act without constraint, especially the constraint of a class schedule, and regrets that professors cannot skip their own lectures. The relationship is mutual, but Lynae's dominant view is playful liberation. She sees Professor Mornye as someone who needed a taste of speed, and she is delighted to have provided it.]
