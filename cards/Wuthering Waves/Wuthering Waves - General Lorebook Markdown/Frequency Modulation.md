## Core Concept

[Frequency Modulation is the process by which frequency waves are actively manipulated, combined, and altered to produce new effects or enhance existing ones. In the context of Wuthering Waves, Frequency Modulation is most concretely expressed through the Modulation mechanic in the game's card-based Echo deployment system, where one Echo is stacked upon another to create a modified, augmented entity. This concept also extends more broadly to the ways Resonators manipulate frequency energy in combat and exploration, shaping raw vibrational forces into targeted techniques, defensive barriers, and environmental interactions.]

## Modulation in Combat Echoes

[Within the tactical card system, Modulation is a core mechanic that allows a Resonator to combine two Echoes into a single, more powerful unit. When Echo A is dragged onto Echo B in the Deployment Zone, Echo A replaces Echo B and inherits Echo B's damage value, which is added to its own original damage. Echo B is then shuffled back into the deck and may be drawn again in later rounds. This process mirrors the broader resonant principle of frequency manipulation, where one waveform overlays and absorbs another, creating constructive interference that amplifies the resulting output. The modulation also activates specific Modulation effects unique to the Echo, providing additional bonuses that depend on the particular skills and attributes of the combined entities.]

## Rules and Constraints

[There are strict constraints governing how Modulation can be performed. Only Echoes held in hand can be used as the modulating source; a deployed Echo cannot be modulated by another deployed Echo, and stacking two deployed Echoes merely swaps their positions rather than combining them. A deployed Echo can be modulated at most twice per engagement. Furthermore, higher-cost Echoes require specific preconditions for modulation, such as stacking a Cost 3 Echo on top of an already-modulated Cost 1 Echo before the Cost 3 can be deployed in modulated form. These rules reflect the underlying physics of frequency manipulation, where stable constructive interference requires careful preparation and cannot be sustained indefinitely.]

## Broader Resonant Applications

[Beyond the card system, the principle of Frequency Modulation underpins many of the abilities that Resonators display in open-world combat and exploration. Resonance Skills, Resonance Liberations, and various elemental techniques all involve the deliberate shaping and modulation of frequency waves to produce specific effects, from freezing enemies with Glacio resonance to unleashing devastating Fusion bursts. The Overclocking Skill and Counterattack mechanics represent advanced forms of real-time modulation, where a Resonator rapidly adjusts their frequency output in response to combat stimuli. Even the process of absorbing and equipping Echoes can be understood as a form of modulation, where the Resonator's own frequency spectrum is modified to incorporate the residual frequencies of defeated enemies.]

## Theoretical Foundations

[The science of Frequency Modulation in Solaris-3 is still being actively studied and refined. Researchers have identified that modulation operates on principles analogous to both constructive wave interference and biological signal transduction, where information is encoded, transmitted, and decoded through frequency patterns. The development of resonant technology such as Resonance Beacons and Resonance Nexuses demonstrates that modulation can be engineered into devices as well as biological systems, expanding the reach of resonant science beyond the innate abilities of individual Resonators. As understanding of frequency manipulation deepens, new applications continue to emerge, promising further advances in both military capability and civilian infrastructure.]
