## The Fractsidus Victim

[Denia represents one of the most tragic dimensions of the ongoing conflict with the Fractsidus, the civilian toll exacted by an organization whose eschatological agenda leaves no room for the lives of ordinary people caught in its path. While many entries in the lorebook focus on combatants, agents, and powerful Resonators who actively shape the course of events, Denia's story is that of someone who was not a willing participant in the cosmic struggle but rather a victim of forces far beyond her control or comprehension. The Fractsidus, in its pursuit of what it perceives as a necessary apocalypse, has consistently demonstrated a willingness to sacrifice civilian lives as collateral damage, or, worse, as deliberate instruments of its plans. Denia's experience as a victim of these operations places a human face on the abstract horror of institutionalized eschatological violence.]

## Life Before and After

[The details of Denia's life before the Fractsidus targeted her remain fragmentary, deliberately so, as the organization's operations are designed to erase the identities and histories of those they consume. What is known is that she was an ordinary person, living an ordinary life, when the machinery of the Fractsidus's plans intersected with her existence and irrevocably altered its trajectory. The nature of the operation that claimed her is classified within the organization's internal records, accessible only to those with sufficient clearance and sufficient disregard for the moral weight of what they would find. What survives are the effects: the displacement, the trauma, the loss of everything that once constituted a coherent life, and the long, difficult process of existing in the aftermath of something that was never meant to have survivors.]

## The Weight of Survival

[Denia's continued existence, her survival of the Fractsidus operation that was meant to consume her, is itself an act of defiance, though not the kind that features in heroic narratives. Survival in this context is quiet, painful, and largely invisible. It consists of small daily choices to continue when the evidence suggests that continuation is pointless. Denia carries within her the knowledge of what the Fractsidus is capable of, what it has already done, and what it plans to do next. This knowledge is a burden that few can share and fewer still can understand. Her presence in the world is a living indictment of the Fractsidus's claim that individual lives are expendable in the service of a greater cosmic purpose, a claim that her survival, against all odds and intentions, silently but eloquently refutes.]
