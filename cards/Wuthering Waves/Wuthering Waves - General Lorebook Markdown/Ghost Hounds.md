## Organizational Profile

[The Ghost Hounds are an international mercenary group whose reputation precedes them across every theater of operation on Solaris-3. Under the leadership of Calcharo, they have built a name synonymous with ruthless efficiency, professional discipline, and uncompromising execution of contracted objectives. The group operates outside the frameworks of national military forces and official security organizations, selling their combat capabilities to whoever can meet their price and survive the consequences of their involvement. Their status as an international operation means they answer to no single government, owe allegiance to no flag, and are bound only by the contracts they accept and the internal code that Calcharo has imposed upon them.]

## Calcharo's Leadership

[Calcharo's leadership defines every aspect of the Ghost Hounds' character and reputation. His personal attributes, ruthless, vengeful, unforgiving, are not merely personality traits but operational principles that shape how the group conducts itself. Under his command, the Ghost Hounds do not merely complete contracts; they make examples, sending messages through the completeness and finality of their work that serve as deterrents to those who might cross them or their clients. Calcharo's Forte ability, which allows him to summon a special Tacet Discord to assist in combat, reflects the same philosophy of using every available tool without regard for convention or restraint. A potential client must be mindful of the price to pay before making him an offer, suggesting that the Ghost Hounds' services come with costs that extend beyond mere payment.]

## Origins and Exile

[The Ghost Hounds' formation connects to Calcharo's exile to the Lawless Zone, a forced relocation that should have destroyed him but instead forged him into something more dangerous than the system that condemned him intended. Within the Zone's crucible of violence and survival, Calcharo built the Ghost Hounds from whatever materials exile provided, recruiting those who had nothing left to lose and training them into a force that ordered societies could not ignore. The group's connection to the Lawless Zone means it draws members from the exiled, the desperate, and the discarded, transforming human refuse into professional soldiers through discipline and shared purpose. This origin story gives the Ghost Hounds a distinctive character among mercenary organizations, their ruthlessness born not from greed but from the understanding that the world has already shown them no mercy.]
