# Xiangli Yao and Mortefi

## Directional Relationship

[Xiangli Yao views Mortefi as an interesting person, and the phrase is more precise than it first appears. Mortefi is not interesting to him merely because he is brilliant, foreign-born, proud, or volatile. He is interesting because his devotion to work has the intensity that can push science toward genuine discovery. Xiangli Yao recognizes in Mortefi a force that is not easily moderated: unwavering determination, fierce pride, and a refusal to settle for shallow results. As a fellow Huaxu Academy scientist, Xiangli Yao sees both the usefulness and the danger of that drive.]

[Their relationship is shaped by a contrast in temperament. Xiangli Yao is gentle, rational, stable, and methodical, a young multi-disciplinary scientist whose authority at the Academy is carried with quiet composure. Mortefi is erudite, aristocratic, fiery, and proud, with Dragon's Breath linking his scientific life to Fusion resonance, anger, and visible bodily risk. Xiangli Yao does not condescend to that volatility. Instead, he treats Mortefi's intensity as part of the energy that makes brilliant discoveries possible. His respect is real, but it is not blind admiration. He understands that talent needs conditions, collaboration, and self-knowledge before it becomes fully realized.]

[The source preserves Mortefi's view of Xiangli Yao as a genius to be outdone, a competitive statement full of irritation and ambition. Xiangli Yao's own perspective absorbs that rivalry without becoming defensive. He knows Mortefi considers him a genius, yet he also says that in a workplace with only geniuses, many ideas will never be realized. This is one of the clearest windows into Xiangli Yao's professional philosophy. He does not believe brilliance alone is sufficient. Research requires people who can carry ideas into form, test them, refine them, and cooperate across specialties.]

[When Xiangli Yao calls Mortefi his dear friend and says Mortefi has yet to grasp his true potential, the relationship becomes more than academic rivalry. It suggests patient confidence. Xiangli Yao sees past Mortefi's competitive pride toward a future in which that force becomes more complete. He does not need to defeat Mortefi's ambition, and he does not answer boast with boast. He positions himself as someone who understands the value of Mortefi's fire while also seeing what Mortefi has not yet understood about himself. From Xiangli Yao's perspective, the relationship is a friendship between scientists where rivalry is real, but mentorship, respect, and hope are stronger.]
