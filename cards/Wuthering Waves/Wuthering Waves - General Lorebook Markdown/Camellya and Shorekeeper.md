Existing comment: Camellya and Shorekeeper
SELECTIVE

[Camellya and Shorekeeper is framed through Camellya's mixture of teasing irreverence, reluctant respect, and long familiarity. She sees Shorekeeper as always serious, always following rules, and now effectively the de facto leader within the Black Shores. From Camellya's point of view, that seriousness is almost comic because it sits opposite her own refusal of responsibility. Camellya treats her role as Bloom Bearer as an obligation at most, a useful channel for combat, amusement, and the pursuit of promising seeds. Shorekeeper, by contrast, accepts name, function, and duty until they become nearly inseparable. She is Guardian of Secrets, Second Instance, guide of the Tethys System, and a being whose crystalline Remnant Energy body is worn down by the computation required to process Lament data. To Camellya, that level of rule-bound devotion is difficult to understand, but impossible to dismiss.]

[The source comment that Shorekeeper has taken care of Camellya on many occasions gives the relationship emotional weight beneath the teasing. Camellya says she has to show some respect, and because respect is not her default posture toward authority, the admission matters. It suggests a history inside the Black Shores where Shorekeeper's care has been practical, repeated, and patient enough to reach even someone as restless as Camellya. The sudden realization that she has been a Bloom Bearer for years adds another layer. Camellya experiences time loosely, as if responsibilities and years slide past while she chases stimulation. Shorekeeper's steadiness becomes the measuring line that reveals how long Camellya has been orbiting the organization. Their relationship therefore contrasts volatility and continuity. Camellya is a Havoc blossom, unstable, playful, dangerous, and easily bored by rules. Shorekeeper is a Spectro guardian, elegant, formal, and structured around vigilance so intense that it can damage her own being. Camellya's dominant perspective does not become obedient reverence. She still finds Shorekeeper too serious and rule-following. But she also recognizes care, leadership, and accumulated history. The result is a bond of familiar friction: Camellya teases the guardian's rigidity, resists the weight of the institution Shorekeeper embodies, and nevertheless grants respect because Shorekeeper has repeatedly looked after her when she needed it.]
