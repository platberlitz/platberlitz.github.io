Existing comment: Hiyuki and Denia
SELECTIVE

[Hiyuki views Denia through guilt before she allows herself any comfort. Denia represents the civilian cost of the Fractsidus's plots, someone pulled into an eschatological design she did not choose and harmed by forces that treat ordinary lives as material for catastrophe. Hiyuki's first instinct is to measure her own failure against that suffering. She believes she failed Denia in a way, because if she had seen through the Fractsidus plot more quickly, Denia might not have endured what she did. That confession fits Hiyuki's severe ethics. As a miko and Special Response Force operative, she does not place responsibility only on villains after harm is done. She asks whether her own delay left space for the harm to happen.]

[The strike she delivered against Denia is the center of her unease. Hiyuki borrowed Rover's strength to carry it out, not to destroy Denia, but to restore a whisper of her true self. The phrasing suggests a rescue that could only be performed through violence, a necessary action whose necessity does not make it emotionally clean. Hiyuki's Glacio discipline and sacred duty allow her to act under pressure, but they do not exempt her from concern over the person struck. Denia was not an enemy in Hiyuki's eyes. She was someone displaced from herself by Fractsidus interference, and the blow was an attempt to break that hold without breaking the person beneath it.]

[Hiyuki's prayer that the act caused no lasting harm reveals the tenderness under her formality. She is told Denia is recovering, but secondhand reassurance is not enough to erase the moral residue of the encounter. If they meet again, Hiyuki intends to apologize in person. That promise keeps the relationship directional: Denia is not a symbol for Hiyuki to resolve privately, but a surviving person owed direct acknowledgment. Hiyuki's view honors Denia's recovery as something separate from Hiyuki's own need for absolution. The apology she imagines is not ceremonial. It is the least she can offer someone whose true self returned through a pain Hiyuki helped inflict.]
