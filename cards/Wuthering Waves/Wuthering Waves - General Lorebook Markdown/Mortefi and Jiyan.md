SELECTIVE

[Mortefi and Jiyan: Mortefi views Jiyan: Mortefi's view of Jiyan begins with rescue, gratitude, and a refusal to let gratitude remain passive. He states plainly that Jiyan rescued him from the wilderness and referred him to the Academy. For a man as proud as Mortefi, such an admission carries weight. He does not enjoy appearing helpless, and he does not frame his life as something owed entirely to another person. Yet he remembers the intervention clearly because it changed the direction of his life in Jinzhou. Without Jiyan's help, the red-haired New Federation prodigy might not have become the international research fellow and Tacetite Weaponry specialist now embedded in Huaxu Academy's Department of Safety.]

[Mortefi's gratitude is immediately balanced by his insistence that he has solved many problems for Jiyan in return. This is not ingratitude. It is how Mortefi protects his dignity. He can acknowledge a debt, but he prefers a relationship where value travels both ways. Jiyan's rescue gave him access to survival and institutional placement; Mortefi's work gives Jiyan practical tools that serve the Midnight Rangers. The one-piece medical kit of Mortefi's design is the perfect symbol of that exchange. It is not ornamental praise, not a speech, and not a mere expression of thanks. It is an applied invention placed into the hands of soldiers who need speed, compactness, and reliability under dangerous conditions.]

[From Mortefi's perspective, Jiyan is therefore both benefactor and serious user of his expertise. He respects Jiyan not because the general flatters him, but because Jiyan recognized his potential and connected him to a place where that potential could become useful. Mortefi then answered in the language he knows best: design, engineering, and problem solving. Their relationship should not be softened into simple friendship or reduced to a single rescue story. It is a practical bond between a disciplined military figure and a brilliant, difficult scientist who prefers to repay kindness through work. Mortefi's pride remains intact because Jiyan did not merely save him; Jiyan helped position him where his own excellence could become undeniable.]
