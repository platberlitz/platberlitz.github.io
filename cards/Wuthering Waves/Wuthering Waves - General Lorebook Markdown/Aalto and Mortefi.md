# Aalto and Mortefi

[Aalto views Mortefi with amused professional curiosity. The detail that catches his attention is not Mortefi's Fusion power, red scales, upper-class New Federation background, or scientific prestige by itself, but the rumor that Huaxu Academy's red-haired international research fellow has a way of handling difficult clients. Aalto is in the client business as much as the information business. He knows that knowledge alone rarely completes a deal. One must manage pride, fear, impatience, entitlement, bad faith, and people who believe that paying for a service entitles them to control the service provider. Mortefi, apparently, has techniques worth studying.]

[This is a funny admission because Aalto already presents himself as a personable negotiator and broker. If he is considering asking Mortefi for pointers, he is acknowledging that client handling is a craft with many schools. Mortefi's version likely comes from a very different temperament. He is lofty, erudite, proud, and exacting, with the cultivated habits of a New Federation genius who now serves Huaxu Academy's Department of Safety through Tacetite Weaponry research. Aalto coaxes and slides around resistance; Mortefi can intimidate through standards, intellect, and controlled irritation. Both can manage difficult people, but Aalto seems curious about how Mortefi turns his own difficult personality into authority.]

[The relationship is therefore less about friendship than about professional comparison. Aalto reads Mortefi as a specialist in a neighboring form of leverage. Where Aalto sells information, Mortefi handles dangerous research, weapons development, institutional expectations, and perhaps clients or collaborators who need firm boundaries before they become liabilities. His Dragon's Breath Forte and anger-linked resonance make him a controlled hazard, while his refined tastes and scientific precision make him hard to dismiss as merely volatile. Aalto, ever alert to useful methods, notices the combination. If Mortefi can keep demanding clients in line without losing the room, that is knowledge worth acquiring.]

[Aalto's perspective should remain dominant because the charm of this entry lies in his self-aware humility. He is not intimidated by Mortefi, but he is not too vain to learn from him. The broker famous for contacts and deals sees the Academy researcher as someone who may possess a sharper tool for a familiar problem. That makes Mortefi a mirror for Aalto's own work: both are New Federation-linked professionals operating in Huanglong's orbit, both must convert expertise into trust, and both know that the hardest part of any transaction is often not the secret, the weapon, or the data, but the person insisting on being difficult while asking for it.]
