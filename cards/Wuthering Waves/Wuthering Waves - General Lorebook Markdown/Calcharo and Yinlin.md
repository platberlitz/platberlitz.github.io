Existing comment: Calcharo and Yinlin
SELECTIVE

[Calcharo and Yinlin is framed through Calcharo's wary recollection of an undercover agent who infiltrated his group. That fact alone would be enough to make most mercenary leaders speak with open hostility, especially one as unforgiving as Calcharo. The Ghost Hounds are built around contracts, discipline, and consequences for betrayal. Infiltration threatens all three. It turns internal trust into vulnerability, forces every operational habit to be reconsidered, and suggests that an outside power believed it could enter Calcharo's organization without paying the price. His huffing response, followed by the warning to take it easy, implies irritation under control. He has not forgotten what Yinlin did, but he also refuses to dramatize it beyond the limits he set. The key line is that he did not give her a hard time, provided she always stayed in her lane.]

[That condition defines Calcharo's perspective. He does not forgive through softness, and he does not trust through optimism. He tolerates when boundaries are understood. Yinlin, as an undercover agent from Huanglong who operates through false identities, covert objectives, Electro resonance, and the puppet Zapstring, represents a mode of power that Calcharo has reason to respect and dislike at the same time. She is precise, adaptable, and dangerous because she can work inside another group's narrative without revealing the hand guiding her. Calcharo likely recognizes the competence required for that kind of infiltration. He also recognizes the risk. His statement that she had to stay in her lane is not merely a personal warning; it is a mercenary command principle. Information gathering may be part of the world, and deception may be useful, but inside the Ghost Hounds there are lines that cannot be crossed without consequence. The relationship remains tense, controlled, and unsentimental. Calcharo's dominant view is not that Yinlin is an enemy to be crushed or an ally to be embraced. She is an operative whose skill has been proven by proximity, whose intrusion has been remembered, and whose continued safety depends on respecting the boundaries he defines. In that sense, his restraint is not mercy in the gentle sense. It is the discipline of a commander who can postpone punishment when order is maintained, while making clear that tolerance has a perimeter.]
