[Lynae views Chisa through the lens of someone who has learned to judge danger quickly, and the first impression matters because it shows how easily her instincts can be overturned by real connection. Chisa's quiet intensity initially reads to Lynae as a warning sign, the kind of controlled silence that makes a former Lawless Zone mercenary think she should keep Rover at a safe distance and avoid making herself too visible. Lynae is used to reading rooms, faces, and exits; her survival before Startorch Academy depended on exactly that. Yet Chisa confounds the expected pattern. Instead of becoming another person Lynae has to evade, Chisa becomes someone she clicks with almost instantly, turning suspicion into interest before Lynae can settle behind her usual jokes and speed.]

[What wins Lynae over is not sentimental warmth, but competence. Chisa takes one look at Lynae's bike and identifies the malfunctioning part faster than any manual diagnostic Lynae could have run. That detail is perfect for Lynae's perspective because her bike is not just transportation. It is movement, independence, escape, and style, all the things that help make her borrowed Academy life feel like something she chose rather than another cage. Chisa understanding the machine so quickly means she is not merely quiet and strange; she is precise, useful, and tuned to hidden structures. That matches Chisa's broader identity as an Ashinoharan student whose perception reaches beneath surfaces, seeing threads and weak points others miss. Lynae may not frame it in academic language, but she recognizes the result immediately.]

[The relationship therefore carries a brisk, friendly charge. Lynae does not need Chisa to be loud to feel compatible with her. If anything, Chisa's stillness gives Lynae something interesting to bounce against. Lynae brings motion, color, reckless confidence, and the desire for a trouble-free campus life; Chisa brings focus, structural insight, and the unnerving calm of someone who notices exactly what is wrong before anyone else has finished asking the question. From Lynae's side, Chisa becomes proof that Startorch Academy can surprise her in good ways. The school is not only schedules, records, and possible exposure. It can also be a place where a girl with a stolen name meets another student who understands machines at a glance and makes Lynae say, with genuine admiration beneath the swagger, that this is impressive.]
