[Although this file is keyed as Jinhsi and Xiangli Yao, the preserved directional POV belongs to Xiangli Yao viewing Jinhsi. He sees Magistrate Jinhsi as a leader who pays close attention to battles against Tacet Discords and repeatedly visits Huaxu Academy to learn about the latest progress in weapons development. From his position as Principal Investigator in charge of academics at the Jinzhou Campus, these visits are not ornamental. They show him a ruler who understands that civic protection depends on research, engineering, field data, and the careful conversion of knowledge into usable defense. Jinhsi does not leave science at a respectful distance. She comes to the Academy herself.]

[The detail that she always seems tense during those visits defines Xiangli Yao's empathy. As a gentle yet rational scientist, he reads that tension as evidence of pressure, not weakness. Jinhsi carries promises and expectations from Jinzhou's citizens, City Hall, Huanglong, and her bond with Sentinel Jué. When she asks about weapons development, she is not indulging technical curiosity. She is asking whether the people under her care can be protected when the next threat arrives. Xiangli Yao recognizes the difference. He understands that every specification, safety margin, and research update can feel like part of the city's future when placed in a Magistrate's hands.]

[This relationship is therefore grounded in institutional service seen from the research side. Xiangli Yao's own life turns private anomaly into public expertise: an Electro Resonator with a prosthetic-linked Forte, a young multi-disciplinary scientist, and an Academy leader responsible for academics and practical innovation. Looking at Jinhsi, he sees another young figure whose personal circumstances have become civic duty. Her tension makes sense to him because responsibility can compress even the strongest people. His respect is measured, observant, and humane. Jinhsi appears to him not as a distant sacred icon, but as a working leader who enters laboratories and development briefings because the promises on her shoulders demand answers from every possible source.]
