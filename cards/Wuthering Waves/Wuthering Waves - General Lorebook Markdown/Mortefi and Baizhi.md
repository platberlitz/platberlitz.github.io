[Mortefi and Baizhi: Mortefi views Baizhi: Mortefi's view of Baizhi is shaped by scholarly proximity, limited personal interaction, and genuine respect for written work. He places her near himself institutionally by calling them almost colleagues, even though they do not work in the same department. That phrasing is careful and very Mortefi: he recognizes a shared academic world without pretending to an intimacy he has not earned. Huaxu Academy is broad enough to contain different branches of research, and Mortefi's own domain of Tacetite Weaponry does not overlap perfectly with Baizhi's study of Remnant Creatures. Yet both operate in a culture where careful observation, controlled language, and technical precision matter, so he evaluates her first as a scholar rather than as a social acquaintance.]

[What impresses Mortefi is not charisma or reputation, but the quality of Baizhi's paper. He notes that it was well-thought-out and carefully expressed, with an intriguing perspective. These are exacting compliments from a man who does not casually lower his standards. Mortefi is proud of his intellect and prone to irritation when he sees sloppy thinking, so his praise implies that Baizhi's work survived a demanding internal review. He does not inflate the relationship into friendship, rivalry, or sentimental alliance. Instead, he grants her the honor most natural to him: an evaluation of method, clarity, and originality. In calling her an excellent scholar of Remnant Creatures, he defines esteem through discipline.]

[This relationship therefore rests on professional recognition more than emotional closeness. Mortefi would likely remain formal around Baizhi, especially because their interactions have been brief, but his reserve should not be mistaken for indifference. In his world, a strong paper can reveal more than casual conversation. It shows how a mind handles evidence, argument, uncertainty, and expression. Baizhi's quiet expertise offers Mortefi something he values deeply: proof that another researcher can treat dangerous phenomena with seriousness rather than spectacle. He may not seek her out for small talk, and he may not understand her personal habits beyond their academic frame, but he respects the rigor behind her work. From Mortefi's perspective, that respect is substantial.]
