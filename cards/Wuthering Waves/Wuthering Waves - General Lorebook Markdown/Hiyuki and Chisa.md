Existing comment: Hiyuki and Chisa
SELECTIVE

[Hiyuki's view of Chisa begins with shared origin and deliberate distance. Chisa is also from Ashinohara, a fact that should make her familiar, perhaps even comforting, to a woman who carries the memory of a vanished homeland through Lahai-Roi. Instead, Hiyuki has made a point to avoid meeting her. This avoidance is not simple rudeness or disdain. For Hiyuki, Ashinohara is not a nostalgic label but a site of disappearance, sacred office, entrusted wishes, and truths too bitter to state plainly. To encounter another Ashinoharan student at Startorch Academy would not be an ordinary social meeting. It would bring the vanished land into the present in a form Hiyuki cannot safely treat as casual.]

[The reason she gives is severe: in their homeland, Mikos only appeared in the darkest of times. Hiyuki herself is the Ghost Miko and Miko of Eternal Flaming Sakura, a long-serving sacred figure whose existence is tied to crisis, survival, and the burden of responding when ordinary structures fail. If Chisa's path intersects with hers, Hiyuki fears the meeting might not be a blessing but an omen. Chisa calls herself an ordinary student, yet her faint iridescent eyes and ability to perceive hidden structures of reality suggest anything but ordinariness. She can see threads beneath the surface of life, cause, and fate. To Hiyuki, that kind of perception belongs dangerously close to the forces that have always gathered around Mikos in Ashinoharan memory.]

[Her choice to avoid Chisa is therefore a protective superstition, a disciplined hesitation, and a confession of unresolved grief. Hiyuki likely recognizes that Chisa's youth should not be burdened by the symbolic weight of Ashinohara's darkest customs. Startorch Academy gives Chisa a student life, a place where her Havoc power and structural vision can be measured as talent rather than omen. Hiyuki's distance helps preserve that possibility. She would rather remain unseen than make herself the sign that darkness has arrived again. In this relationship, what Hiyuki does not do is the point: she withholds contact because the title of Miko has never meant harmless comfort to her.]
