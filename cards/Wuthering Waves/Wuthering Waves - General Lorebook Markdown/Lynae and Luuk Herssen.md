Existing comment: Lynae and Luuk Herssen
SELECTIVE

[Lynae views Luuk Herssen with the wary humor of someone who knows exactly how dangerous it can be to be seen too clearly. Her first meeting with Dr. Herssen happens at the infirmary, a place that should be safe by definition, but Lynae's reaction is not simple comfort. She gets the strange feeling that he can see right through her. For most students, that might mean an unusually perceptive doctor noticing exhaustion, stress, or a concealed injury. For Lynae, it carries much higher stakes. Her entire Startorch Academy life rests on a stolen identity, a name taken from a Fractsidus operative so she could escape the Lawless Zone and live as a student.]

[Luuk's ability to unsettle her makes sense within his own profile. He is the head physician and counselor of the Resonator Nursing Unit, a Spectro Resonator whose Forte is Phase Transition and whose past is described through blood and lies. He is warm, competent, and trusted by students, but his kindness is paired with diagnostic sharpness. He has made a vocation out of opening symptoms, masks, and deceptions to reach the truth beneath. From Lynae's perspective, a doctor like that is both useful and risky. He may be exactly the adult who can tell when she needs help, and exactly the adult who might notice the cracks in the official Lynae record if he looked too hard.]

[Her response is to pivot toward the practical benefit: Dr. Herssen writes very generous doctor's note slips. The joke is a shield, but it is not empty. Those notes matter to a student whose relationship with class schedules is already fragile and whose Academy life depends on balancing freedom with institutional compliance. A generous doctor's note can become a small tool of survival, giving Lynae room to breathe without making every absence a disciplinary trail. The relationship is therefore built on mutual restraint. Lynae suspects Luuk sees more than he says. Luuk, if he does notice more, appears to choose care over exposure. Lynae's perspective keeps the tone breezy because that is how she protects herself, but underneath the breeziness is a precise recognition: Dr. Herssen is the kind of person who could threaten her cover, yet he has so far made her life easier. That combination earns unease, gratitude, and cautious trust.]
