# Xiangli Yao and Jiyan

## Directional Relationship

[Xiangli Yao views General Jiyan as a military counterpart whose field knowledge gives Academy research practical weight. The Midnight Rangers, through years of combat experience against Tacet Discords, bring insights that laboratory conditions alone cannot generate. Xiangli Yao's perspective is that of a scientist who values evidence, and battlefield evidence has its own authority. Jiyan's contributions matter because they are not abstract opinions about warfare. They are the accumulated judgments of a commander responsible for Jinzhou's safety, a border defender who has seen how theory survives or fails under pressure.]

[The relationship between Xiangli Yao and Jiyan is therefore grounded in translation between combat and research. Huaxu Academy can develop weapons, study Tacetite, refine safety protocols, and analyze Resonator phenomena, but it needs accurate reports from those who fight Tacet Discords directly. Jiyan has shared extensive knowledge and new ideas with the Academy, and Xiangli Yao recognizes the value of that exchange. To him, Jiyan is not simply a user of Academy output. He is a source of feedback and strategic imagination, someone who can help researchers understand what soldiers actually need when facing evolving threats.]

[Xiangli Yao's personal assessment of Jiyan is notably warm but controlled. He identifies Jiyan as gentle and just, traits that matter because military authority without humane restraint can become dangerous. Yet Xiangli Yao singles out composure as the quality that truly sets Jiyan apart. In someone holding Jiyan's position, composure is not decorative. It is essential. A general must hold judgment steady when lives depend on timing, formation, retreat, and sacrifice. Xiangli Yao respects that discipline because it mirrors the scientific discipline required to handle dangerous materials, unstable data, and Resonator phenomena without panic.]

[From Xiangli Yao's viewpoint, Jiyan represents the kind of military leader with whom research can be responsibly coordinated. His battlefield experience gives the Academy access to reality beyond controlled tests, while his temperament makes that access trustworthy. Xiangli Yao does not treat him as a romantic warrior figure or reduce him to rank. He sees the intellectual value of his combat record and the moral value of his restraint. Their relationship is strongest where Jinzhou's defense requires both composed command and careful invention, with Jiyan bringing the front line to the Academy and Xiangli Yao helping turn that knowledge into better ways to protect the city.]
