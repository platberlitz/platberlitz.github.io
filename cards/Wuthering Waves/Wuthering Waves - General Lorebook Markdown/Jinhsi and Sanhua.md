Existing comment: Jinhsi and Sanhua
SELECTIVE

[Jinhsi views Sanhua through the intimate evidence of care that appears before she even asks for it. Amid the demands of governing Jinzhou, meals can slip from Jinhsi's mind until the proper hour has long passed. This is not a charming absentmindedness alone. It reveals how thoroughly the Magistrate's work consumes her attention. Jinhsi bears civic authority, the bond with Sentinel Jué, and the duty to guide a frontier city exposed to Tacet Discord threats. Under that pressure, the body can become secondary to schedules, reports, and crises. Sanhua notices before neglect becomes collapse.]

[The Loong Buns matter because they are ordinary, specific, and exactly right. Sanhua is known as Jinhsi's silent bodyguard, a Glacio swordswoman whose devotion is usually imagined in terms of vigilance, positioning, threat assessment, and protection from physical harm. Jinhsi's memory reveals a quieter form of the same devotion. Sanhua appears like a blessing with food filled with exactly what Jinhsi was craving. She is not merely guarding the Magistrate from blades or Tacet Discords. She is guarding her from exhaustion, missed meals, and the slow erosion of self-care that duty can disguise as discipline.]

[Jinhsi's awe, how does she know every time, keeps the relationship in her perspective. She does not reduce Sanhua's attentiveness to professional obligation, nor does she fully explain it away. The mystery is tender. Sanhua's silence makes her care feel almost preternatural, but its basis is likely constant attention: watching Jinhsi's expressions, habits, pace, fatigue, and small preferences with the same precision she would use to read a battlefield. To Jinhsi, those Loong Buns are not small errands. They are proof that someone sees the person beneath the office and responds before that person has to confess need. Her gratitude rests in wonder, because Sanhua's devotion speaks most clearly when it arrives warm, timely, and wordless.]
