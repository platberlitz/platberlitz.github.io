[Mortefi and Calcharo: Calcharo views Mortefi: Calcharo's opinion of Mortefi begins in open dislike. To him, Mortefi appears pretentious, arrogant, and self-absorbed, the living image of the snooty New Federation noble class that Calcharo has every reason to distrust. Calcharo's own history came through exile, the Lawless Zone, and mercenary survival, so Mortefi's refinement does not read as harmless elegance. It reads as privilege hardened into personality. The lab coat, cultured tastes, educated speech, and pride of an international research fellow all evoke the social world Calcharo survived outside of, a world that could afford polish because others endured the broken ground beneath it.]

[Yet Calcharo's contempt is not blind. He is too practical a commander to ignore competence when it is real. Mortefi's talent in crafting Tacetite Weapons forces Calcharo to separate the man from the work, or at least to admit that the work has value. That distinction fits Calcharo's mercenary ethic. Personal distaste does not erase utility, and a bad personality does not make a good weapon less effective. As leader of the Ghost Hounds, Calcharo thinks in terms of contracts, equipment, risk, and battlefield consequence. If Mortefi's designs can improve arms, protect personnel, or change the reliability of a job, then the arrogant scholar becomes relevant despite himself.]

[Calcharo's offer to arrange a meeting with a trusted arms dealer is therefore a measured professional bridge, not an olive branch of affection. He would not flatter Mortefi for comfort, and he would not pretend to enjoy his company. The offer says that Calcharo recognizes a possible exchange: Mortefi brings scientific talent, the dealer brings channels of supply and demand, and Calcharo gains the satisfaction of seeing useful capability placed where it can matter. His perspective remains dominant and unsentimental. Mortefi may embody everything Calcharo dislikes about privileged New Federation refinement, but he is also a serious Tacetite Weaponry expert. Calcharo can despise the posture and still respect the product. In his world, that is already a meaningful form of acknowledgment.]
