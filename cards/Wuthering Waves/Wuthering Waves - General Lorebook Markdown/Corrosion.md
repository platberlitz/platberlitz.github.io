## Nature of Corrosion

[Corrosion is the progressive physical deterioration of a Resonator's body that results from excessive and sustained exposure to their own resonant frequencies. Unlike Reverberation, which is a feedback effect that manifests as acute episodes of disorientation and distress, Corrosion is a slow, cumulative process that erodes the Resonator's physical integrity over time. It is the long-term cost of wielding resonance, a price that every Resonator pays simply by existing as a conduit for frequency waves. The more powerful the Resonator and the more intensively they exercise their abilities, the greater the risk and severity of Corrosion.]

## Biological Mechanism

[At the biological level, Corrosion occurs because the Resonator's cells and tissues are not perfectly adapted to channeling high-frequency energy indefinitely. Each act of resonance, from the smallest manipulation of elemental frequencies to the most devastating Resonance Liberation, subjects the body to vibrational stress that causes microdamage at the cellular level. Over time, this microdamage accumulates, leading to tissue degradation, weakened immune function, and accelerated aging. The process is particularly severe in Resonators who specialize in high-output elements such as Fusion and Havoc, whose frequencies carry greater inherent destructive potential and therefore impose greater strain on the body.]

## Symptoms and Progression

[The early stages of Corrosion are often subtle and easy to overlook. Resonators may notice reduced stamina, slower recovery times, and a gradual decline in their ability to sustain high-frequency output. As Corrosion progresses, more severe symptoms emerge, including chronic pain, loss of fine motor control, and visible physical changes such as skin discoloration, hair thinning, and the appearance of resonant scars, which are permanent marks left where the frequency waves have literally eroded the flesh. In advanced stages, Corrosion can lead to organ failure, neurological degradation, and ultimately death. The progression is not uniform, however, as individual Resonators vary widely in their natural resilience and their ability to mitigate the effects through training, rest, and resonant therapy.]

## Relationship to Echo Absorption

[The process of absorbing Echoes, while beneficial for expanding a Resonator's power, also contributes to Corrosion. When a Resonator integrates a foreign frequency into their own Frequency Spectrum, they are essentially forcing their body to accommodate vibrational patterns that were originally native to a completely different organism. This integration process, while remarkably effective in the short term, introduces additional stress on the body and can accelerate the onset of Corrosion if too many or too disparate Echoes are absorbed in a short period. Resonance Resistance, a property that increases resistance to all attribute damage by up to 200 percent, is one mechanism by which the body attempts to defend itself against the corrosive effects of foreign frequencies, but it is not a complete shield.]

## Treatment and Prognosis

[There is currently no known cure for Corrosion, but a range of treatments exist to slow its progression and manage its symptoms. Resonance Nexuses provide therapeutic stabilization for Resonators suffering from acute Corrosion episodes, using targeted frequency therapy to realign the damaged tissue and reduce vibrational stress. Long-term management strategies include careful monitoring of resonance exposure, regular rest periods, dietary modifications, and the use of specialized resonant equipment designed to reduce the strain on the body. Despite these measures, Corrosion remains an inescapable reality for all Resonators, a constant reminder that the power they wield comes at a cost that is ultimately measured in the very substance of their lives.]
