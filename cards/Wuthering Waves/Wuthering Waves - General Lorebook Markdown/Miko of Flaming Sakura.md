## Sacred Tradition

[The Miko of Flaming Sakura is a sacred title and spiritual duty originating from Ashinohara, the vanished land whose traditions survive primarily through this single office and its current holder. The miko tradition encompasses shrine maiden practices that position the holder as an intermediary between the human world and forces of divine or supernatural significance. In Ashinohara, the Miko tended a sacred flame said to have been ignited and maintained by divine hands, a responsibility that carried both practical spiritual functions and deep symbolic meaning about the continuity of sacred tradition across generations.]

## Hiyuki's Sacred Duty

[Hiyuki bears the full weight of the Miko of Flaming Sakura tradition as its last living practitioner. Her exile from Ashinohara, whether by the land's destruction or other circumstances, has not absolved her of the duties attached to her title. She continues to perform the miko's functions even in the frozen north of Lahai-Roi, where she serves as the sole member of the Special Response Force. The Flaming Sakura designation connects to the cherry blossom, a symbol of beauty and transience in many traditions, and the combination of flame and flower imagery suggests a spiritual practice that embraces both power and impermanence.]

## Transformation and Continuity

[The Miko tradition has undergone significant transformation through Hiyuki's relocation and changing circumstances. In Ashinohara, the miko's duties were embedded within a supportive community and landscape. In Lahai-Roi, Hiyuki must maintain these practices alone, adapting ancient rituals to an environment fundamentally different from the one that shaped them. Her Glacio resonance, expressed through sword combat, represents a transformation of the original fire traditions into a form suited to the frozen north. Yet the core responsibility remains unchanged, to carry forward the wishes of previous generations, to maintain the connection between worlds that the sacred flame once physically embodied, and to ensure that Ashinohara's spiritual legacy does not die with its land.]
