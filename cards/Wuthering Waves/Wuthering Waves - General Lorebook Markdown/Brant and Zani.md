Existing comment: Brant and Zani
SELECTIVE

[Brant and Zani contains mutual remarks in the source, but the dossier keeps Brant's view dominant because Brant is the primary key and the first named perspective. He looks at Zani and immediately notices authenticity beneath the roles she has carried. Many people wear masks to fit in, and Brant, of all people, does not say that as a simple criticism. He is an actor, captain, and performer who knows masks can be useful, beautiful, and even truthful when chosen freely. What impresses him about Zani is that she does not seem trapped by the masks expected of her. She is a Mutant Spectro Resonator, a reliable Averardo Bank employee, a Montelli-affiliated professional, a woman marked by horns, tail, discipline, punctuality, and the nocturnal force of the Blazing Nightwalker. Any one of those identities could become a cage. Brant instead sees someone who has passed through past roles and arrived in the present with self-possession intact. Whatever her history, whatever parts she has played, she is here now and true to herself.]

[That judgment reveals Brant's own values as much as Zani's character. The Troupe of Fools lives by chosen freedom, not by the erasure of obligation, and Brant respects people who can inhabit difficult structures without surrendering their core. Zani works inside the ordered machinery of the Montelli family's financial world, where paperwork, vault records, schedules, and social hierarchy shape daily life. Brant, whose own life looks almost enviably free by comparison, recognizes that her steadiness is not dullness. It is a form of resistance and control. The source also preserves Zani's reciprocal view, and it sharpens the contrast: she sees Brant as a skilled actor, respected captain, and fighter willing to sacrifice everything for truth and justice, while envying his apparent freedom from stacks of paperwork. Her joke about joining the Troupe of Fools if she knew anything about acting is playful, but it also confirms why Brant's life appears attractive from her position. Still, the relationship is not a symmetric biography. Brant's dominant perception is that Zani's authenticity matters more than the roles around her. He admires her because she does not need to imitate his carefree style to be free. Her freedom is quieter, stricter, and more work-worn, but to Brant it is real enough to command respect.]
