Existing comment: Camellya and Jinhsi
SELECTIVE

[Camellya and Jinhsi is framed by Camellya's impatience with self-sacrificial duty. She looks at Jinhsi, the Loong Maiden of Jinzhou, and immediately compares her to Shorekeeper because both appear devoted to obligations that consume the self. Jinhsi is Magistrate, Sentinel Jue's appointed Resonator, and a young ruler whose Spectro power, civic office, and draconic symbolism all bind her to Jinzhou's survival. To many, that devotion is noble and reassuring. To Camellya, it is bewildering. She sees the burden more than the honor, the self-denial more than the glory. Her comment about all the self-sacrifice is not respectful praise in the conventional sense. It carries exasperation from someone who refuses shackles, dislikes responsibility, and pursues amusement, battle, and fate on her own terms.]

[This reaction reveals Camellya's value system with unusual clarity. She is not blind to Jinhsi's strength. A person charged with defending a frontier city and carrying a Sentinel bond clearly has power, resolve, and symbolic weight. But Camellya's interest does not settle on the grandeur of the office. It catches on the emotional and existential cost. Why burden themselves so much? The question is almost accusatory, but also genuinely confused. Camellya can understand desire, obsession, challenge, and the thrill of pursuing a promising opponent. What she cannot easily understand is choosing to make oneself a vessel for public expectation, especially when that expectation demands endless care for others. Jinhsi's life of reflection, humility, and city-centered altruism therefore resembles a cage to Camellya, just as Shorekeeper's guardianship does. The relationship remains directional through Camellya's gaze: Jinhsi becomes a mirror in which Camellya sees the kind of devotion she resists. That does not make the view shallow. Camellya's rejection of responsibility is tied to her own unstable Havoc resonance, artificial classification, and body altered by bloom-like danger. She lives as if freedom must be seized before duty can root around the throat. Looking at Jinhsi, she recognizes a powerful woman who chose burden anyway, and that choice unsettles her more than any title or battlefield display.]
