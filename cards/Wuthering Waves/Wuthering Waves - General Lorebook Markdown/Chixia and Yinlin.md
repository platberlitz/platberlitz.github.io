## Directional Relationship

[Directional POV: Chixia views Yinlin. Chixia remembers Yinlin as the thunder that once lit up her path ahead, a striking image from a Fusion Patroller describing an Electro undercover agent. Yinlin's power, covert competence, and sharp presence made her an aspirational figure for Chixia. At one time, Chixia wanted to be like her, which means Yinlin functioned as more than a colleague or distant operative. She represented a model of strength that seemed capable of illuminating the way forward with dramatic force, like lightning revealing a road in darkness.]

[Chixia's later realization, that not everyone has that power, gives the relationship its maturity. She does not simply abandon admiration for Yinlin. She grows beyond imitation. Yinlin belongs to the world of intelligence work, cover identities, psychological resilience, and precise Electro control through Zapstring. That kind of thunder is powerful, but it is not the only valid way to protect people. Chixia's own life in the Public Security Bureau teaches a different scale of service: patrol routes, children, street trouble, local trust, and immediate help. Her Gallant Blaze may be smaller in her own words, but it is close to the people around her.]

[The phrase "a small flicker of flame" is Chixia's self-acceptance. It could sound modest to the point of underestimating herself, but in context it is liberating. She no longer measures her worth by whether she can become thunder like Yinlin. She is happy doing what she can, where she can, for the people nearby. This is not a rejection of Yinlin's importance. It is a recognition that inspiration should lead to one's own form of service rather than a borrowed identity. Yinlin lights the path, and Chixia chooses the flame she can personally tend.]

[This relationship should keep Chixia's perspective dominant. Yinlin is not profiled as a full undercover agent here, but as the figure Chixia once looked up to and later learned to differ from. The emotional arc runs from awe, to attempted imitation, to grounded self-knowledge. Chixia's respect remains, but it no longer makes her feel insufficient. By accepting herself as a small flicker, she defines heroism at human scale: not the thunder that dominates the sky, but the steady flame that helps the person standing next to it.]
