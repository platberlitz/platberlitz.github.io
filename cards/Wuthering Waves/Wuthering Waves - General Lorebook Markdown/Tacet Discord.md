## Origin and Nature

[Tacet Discords, commonly abbreviated as TDs, are hostile entities that emerged across the surface of Solaris-3 in the aftermath of the Lament. They are not natural organisms in any conventional biological sense but rather manifestations of corrupted resonance frequency, entities born from the distortion of the world's fundamental harmonic order. When a Tacet Discord is defeated, the residual frequency it leaves behind may crystallize into an Echo, a phenomenon that Resonators can absorb to gain Data Bank experience and unlock combat abilities. This process of frequency crystallization is central to both the threat and the utility that Tacet Discords represent: they are simultaneously the greatest danger facing the civilizations of Solaris-3 and the primary source of the enhanced abilities that allow Resonators to fight back against them.]

## Classification System

[Tacet Discords are categorized by a tiered classification system that reflects their threat level, complexity, and combat capability. The system ranges from Class I TDs, the smallest and most numerous variants, to Class IV TDs, which represent the most powerful and dangerous specimens encountered in the world. Class I TDs, designated as one-cost entities in Resonator combat terminology, include creatures such as the Vanguard Junrock, Fission Junrock, Electro Predator, Fusion Warrior, Havoc Warrior, Snip Snap, Zig Zag, Whiff Whaff, Tick Tack, Glacio Predator, Gulpuff, Cruisewing, Sabyr Boar, Excarat, Hoartoise, Fusion Dreadmane, Hooscamp, Diamondclaw, Chirpuff, Traffic Illuminator, Clang Bang, Lava Larva, and Dwarf Cassowary. These entities are typically small, relatively simple in behavior, and encountered in large numbers across the wilderness of Solaris-3. Despite their lower individual threat, they pose significant danger through sheer numbers and their tendency to congregate in contaminated zones.]

[Class II TDs are not widely documented in available records, suggesting either a gap in classification or a transitional tier that merges with Class III designations. Class III TDs, designated as three-cost entities, represent a significant escalation in both power and behavioral complexity. Notable Class III specimens include the Violet-Feathered Heron, Cyan-Feathered Heron, Stonewall Bracer, Flautist, Tambourinist, Chasm Guardian, Rocksteady Guardian, Viridblaze Saurian, Roseshroom, Spearback, Havoc Dreadmane, Hoochief Cyclone, Carapace, Autopuppet Scout, Glacio Dreadmane, Lumiscale Construct, and Lightcrusher. These entities demonstrate more sophisticated attack patterns, greater resilience, and often possess area-of-effect or support capabilities that make them considerably more dangerous than their Class I counterparts.]

## Class IV TDs and Boss-Level Entities

[Class IV TDs, designated as four-cost entities, represent the apex of Tacet Discord threat and are commonly referred to as boss-level enemies. These are the most powerful and dangerous Tacet Discords encountered on Solaris-3, each possessing unique combat mechanics, devastating attack patterns, and the ability to reshape the battlefield around them. Prominent Class IV TDs include the Crownless, a humanoid entity that requires Counterattacks and Dodge Counters to interrupt; Tempest Mephis and Thundering Mephis, storm-wielding behemoths; the Impermanence Heron, a massive avian entity; the Inferno Rider, a flame-shrouded mounted combatant; Feilian Beringal, a wind-manipulating adversary; Lampylumen Myriad, an ice-based terror; Mourning Aix, a light-aligned phantom; the Mech Abomination, a mechanical monstrosity; the Bell-Borne Geochelone, a colossal armored entity; and the Dreamless, a being of profound and unsettling power. Each Class IV TD drops unique Echoes upon defeat that can be equipped by Resonators to gain powerful combat skills.]

## Special Variants and the Threnodian

[Beyond the standard classification tiers, certain extraordinary Tacet Discords exist that defy conventional categorization. Phantom Tacet Discords are extremely rare mutated variants that appear randomly across the world of Solaris-3, distinguished by their altered appearances and the unique Phantom Echoes they yield upon defeat. Their scarcity has earned them the colloquial name "Phantoms" among Resonators. Outlier Tacet Discords represent another special category; these entities cannot be engaged in combat until a Resonator has achieved a sufficient Data Hub level, suggesting that they exist at a threshold of power or frequency that requires advanced equipment or resonance attunement to perceive and confront. Most enigmatic of all are the entities bearing the designation Threnodian, referenced in Echo records associated with specimens such as the Threnodian Leviathan and the Threnodian Voidborne Construct. The Threnodian designation appears to represent either an entirely separate category of Tacet Discord or a classification above and beyond the standard Class IV tier, possibly indicating entities with a direct connection to the Lament's origin or to forces operating at a cosmic scale beyond the immediate catastrophe. The existence of such entities suggests that the full depth and nature of the Tacet Discord phenomenon remains only partially understood, and that greater threats may yet lie beyond the current boundaries of human knowledge on Solaris-3.]

## Ecological Role and Contamination

[Tacet Discords do not merely exist as isolated combat encounters; they form an integral part of the corrupted ecology that the Lament has imposed upon Solaris-3. In Lament Residue Zones, TDs affected by Irideglow contamination exhibit heightened aggression and altered behavior patterns, suggesting that the Lament's residual energy continues to actively modify and escalate the Tacet Discord threat over time. The presence of organized enemy factions such as the Exiles and the Fractsidus, who field their own combatants alongside or in competition with wild Tacet Discords, adds a layer of strategic complexity to the post-Lament world. The Fractsidus in particular deploy specialized units including Cannoneers, Executioners, and Gunmasters, indicating that some factions have learned to harness or coexist with the Tacet Discord phenomenon rather than simply opposing it. The ongoing struggle against the Tacet Discords represents not merely a military campaign but a fundamental contest for the future of Solaris-3 itself, as each cleared Tacet Field and each absorbed Echo represents a small reclamation of the world's lost harmony from the discord that the Lament unleashed upon it.]
