# Tranquilite

## Definition

[Tranquilite-type Tacet Discords mimic earth, stone, minerals, and other inorganic natural matter. The source entry places them in a category distinct from living plants and animals, humanlike forms, and man-made objects. Their bodies are frequency-born imitations of nonliving natural forms. This makes the class important for describing post-Lament terrain that may not be as inert as it appears. A stone shape, mineral mass, or earthen body can be a Reverberation Body rather than ordinary geology.]

## Inorganic Natural Mimicry

[The phrase inorganic natural matter gives Tranquilites a precise boundary. They are not Staticoids, because Staticoids mimic or parasitize artificial objects. They are not Howlers, because Howlers imitate flora or fauna. They are not Whisperin or Murmurin, because those classes turn toward humanlike traces. Tranquilites imitate the nonliving materials of the natural world: earth, stone, mineral, and related matter. Their mimicry can make danger feel quiet, heavy, and environmental, as if the landscape itself has learned to carry chaotic frequency.]

## Still Tacet Discords

[A Tranquilite should not be mistaken for an animated rock in a generic sense. As a Tacet Discord, it is formed from chaotic frequencies after the Lament and sustained by resonance. Its mineral or stone resemblance belongs to its Reverberation Body, not to a normal geological history. The source-aligned distinction is useful: the thing may look like terrain, but its existence belongs to the same frequency ecology as other Tacet Discords. It consumes frequencies and can leave traces that connect to Echoes and Remnant Energy after decay.]

## Dossier Use

[Tranquilite dossiers should highlight the instability of supposedly solid surroundings. A field report might describe earth that moves, mineral shapes that attack, or stone forms that prove to be Tacet Discord bodies. The point is not to invent a separate species of rock creature, but to show how chaotic frequency can imitate inorganic nature. This class is especially useful when the scene needs quiet menace: the ground, cliff, ruin, or mineral deposit may appear stable until its frequency-born body reveals itself as part of the post-Lament disorder.]
