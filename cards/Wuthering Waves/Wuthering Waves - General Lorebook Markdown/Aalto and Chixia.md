# Aalto and Chixia

[Aalto's view of Chixia begins with a small admission of social miscalculation: their first conversation did not go quite as planned, though he considers himself lucky that they worked it out later. For a man who sells information through charm, timing, and careful emotional reading, that is a revealing confession. Chixia is not a hidden power broker or an academic skeptic, but a junior Patroller of Jinzhou's Public Security Bureau, direct, warm, energetic, and publicly devoted to helping ordinary citizens. Aalto's usual evasive ease can easily land badly with someone whose job is to protect a city from troublemakers, scams, and suspicious outsiders.]

[From his perspective, Chixia represents a type of person he cannot handle only as a customer or source. She is civic heat: visible, fast-moving, and grounded in the alleys and daily rhythms of Jinzhou. Aalto's information trade often benefits from ambiguity, but Chixia operates in an environment where ambiguity can look like danger. If their first conversation faltered, it likely did so because his polished broker habits met her Patroller instincts. Aalto might have expected curiosity, leverage, or banter; Chixia may have heard evasion where he intended charm. The fact that he remembers the awkwardness suggests that her reaction mattered enough to interrupt his normal confidence.]

[The later repair is equally important. Aalto does not describe Chixia as gullible, troublesome, or beneath him. He says they worked it out, which implies that his view of her settled into something more respectful after the initial mismatch. Chixia's cheerfulness is not naivety. Her Fusion power, pistol training, and stable public service make her someone who can respond to danger without losing her approachable warmth. Aalto, who notices how people move through networks, would understand the value of a Patroller beloved by the city. She hears things because people trust her. She sees things because she is present when ordinary lives are disrupted.]

[This relationship should stay centered on Aalto's perspective because it shows how his broker persona changes when faced with straightforward civic sincerity. Chixia is not primarily a business asset here, even if her position would make her a useful source. She is a reminder that not every conversation can be steered by smoke, jokes, and implied deals. Aalto's luck in working things out reveals adaptability rather than conquest. With Chixia, he has to respect the open flame of public duty, and that respect makes the relationship more interesting than a simple failed pitch.]
