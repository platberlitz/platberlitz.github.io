# Aemeath and Mornye

[Aemeath's view of Mornye begins with a first meeting rather than a long catalog of academic credentials. She says the first time she met Professor Mornye was when Rover took her to the Academy, right before Rover left Lahai-Roi. The source fragment ends there, but the timing already carries emotional force. Aemeath, whose existence after overclocking leaves her displaced from ordinary visibility, remembers being brought to Startorch Academy and introduced to a professor at the edge of Rover's departure. The meeting is therefore not only about Mornye as a person; it is about a threshold moment, when Aemeath's world was being reoriented around the Academy, absence, and the fragile continuity of care.]

[Mornye's existing dossier gives that meeting additional weight. She is a Fusion Resonator, Spacetrek Collective researcher, and Startorch Academy professor of Exostrider Engineering, a field directly tied to the protector entity that shaped Aemeath's fate. She was once a Startorch student herself and later became one of the institution's senior minds, quiet, exceptionally intelligent, and driven by the dream of surpassing space-time to see the world beyond in all its beauty. From Aemeath's perspective, meeting Mornye would not feel like meeting an ordinary faculty member. It would mean encountering someone whose research world touches Exostrider, the Academy's memory, and the grand limits Aemeath crossed in the most painful way.]

[The mention of Rover taking Aemeath there matters because Aemeath often understands people through the traces they leave around Rover and Lahai-Roi's turning points. Rover's impending departure casts the meeting in a tender, unsettled light. Aemeath is being connected to someone who may remain when Rover leaves. Mornye, as professor, researcher, and former student, represents institutional continuity: a person rooted in the Academy before disaster and still working within it after. For Aemeath, who cannot rely on ordinary embodiment or ordinary social presence, that kind of continuity may feel both comforting and distant. Mornye belongs to rooms, departments, reports, and classes that Aemeath can approach only from the strange angle of a ghost.]

[This relationship should remain directional because its lore value is Aemeath's memory of introduction, not a symmetrical account of mentor and student. Mornye appears in Aemeath's view as a quiet academic figure encountered at a moment of transition. The meeting suggests possibility: perhaps understanding, perhaps Exostrider-related knowledge, perhaps simply another adult within Startorch who could help hold the world together after Rover left. Because the source breaks off, the dossier should preserve uncertainty rather than invent a full mentorship. What can be said is enough: Aemeath remembers Professor Mornye as part of the Academy threshold, where personal departure, research authority, and the aftermath of Exostrider converged.]
