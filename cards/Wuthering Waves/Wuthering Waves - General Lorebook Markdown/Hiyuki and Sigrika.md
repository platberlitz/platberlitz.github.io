Existing comment: Hiyuki and Sigrika
SELECTIVE

[Hiyuki views Sigrika first as a child carrying too much. The source preserves her judgment in incomplete form, but its emotional direction is clear: Sigrika bears an awful load for someone of her age and seeks to shoulder the expectations placed upon a Solsworn. Hiyuki's response comes from someone who understands the danger of sacred or public expectation settling on the young before they have learned how to separate duty from self-erasure. Her own life as Ghost Miko and Miko of Eternal Flaming Sakura has been shaped by entrusted wishes, a vanished homeland, and more than a century of service. When she looks at Sigrika, she recognizes the early outline of a burden that can define a person before that person has chosen its meaning.]

[Sigrika's Startorch Academy profile makes that burden more poignant. She is not merely an abstract bearer of expectation, but an Academy student and top-tier Aero Resonator whose combat rhythm involves sun and moon runes, plushie-like constructs, Echo damage, and careful execution. The surface can appear bright, even whimsical. Hiyuki looks past the softness of those motifs to the pressure underneath. A child praised for capability can quickly become a vessel for other people's hopes, fears, and demands. The word Solsworn implies a vow, a sacred alignment, or an inherited role that does not leave much room for ordinary uncertainty. Hiyuki knows how easily reverence can become weight.]

[Her perspective is not dismissive of Sigrika's strength. Hiyuki would not deny that Sigrika is talented, disciplined, and capable of remarkable feats within the Academy's Resonator environment. What troubles her is the expectation that talent should automatically become self-sacrifice. She sees a girl trying to be worthy of a title or role, and Hiyuki's compassion lies in noticing the cost before the world finishes praising the performance. The relationship remains directional through Hiyuki's grave attention: Sigrika is someone to be protected from the full cruelty of symbolic responsibility, even if she must still grow into power. Hiyuki's concern is the concern of a miko who knows that sacred burdens can look beautiful from the outside while quietly consuming the one who bears them.]
