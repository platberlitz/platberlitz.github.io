# Aalto and Calcharo

[Aalto's view of Calcharo is built around business clarity. He enjoys dealing with a Ghost Hound because, in his words, they keep things clean, stay straightforward, and do not fool around. Coming from Aalto, that is a serious compliment hidden inside casual phrasing. He is an information broker who spends much of his professional life among half-truths, evasions, inflated promises, frightened clients, and people who want secrets without paying the real cost. The Ghost Hounds are dangerous, but their danger is legible. Calcharo's mercenary structure is founded on contracts, fair price, hard terms, and consequences for betrayal. Aalto can work with that because the rules are sharp enough to trust.]

[Calcharo's reputation would make many contacts cautious, but Aalto reads reputation as a tool rather than a reason to panic. The man called Phantom Hunters came out of the New Federation's Lawless Zone and turned survival into command over an international mercenary group. That history signals brutality, but it also signals competence. Aalto's preference for clean business does not mean moral innocence; it means he values people who state the job, accept the price, and understand what happens if a bargain is broken. With Calcharo, Aalto does not have to waste energy separating empty bravado from actual capability. The Ghost Hounds' methods may be ruthless, but their professionalism makes them easier to navigate than a smiling amateur with no discipline.]

[The reciprocal note from Calcharo reinforces the same practical trust. Calcharo can hand someone Aalto's contact and even offer a twenty percent discount if his name is mentioned. That is not sentimental intimacy; it is professional endorsement. Calcharo would not casually direct people toward an unreliable broker, and Aalto would not praise Ghost Hound dealings if their contracts created unnecessary mess. Each recognizes the other's usefulness inside a dangerous market. Aalto supplies information, introductions, routes, and leverage. Calcharo supplies force, discipline, and the kind of mercenary certainty that makes clients think twice before playing games.]

[This dossier should not soften them into friendly opposites. Aalto remains the dominant lens: he evaluates Calcharo as a broker evaluates a client, partner, and armed institution. His respect comes from transaction design, not from admiration for violence itself. Calcharo matters to him because a clean mercenary is safer than a vague ally, and because the Ghost Hounds understand the language of price as fluently as Aalto understands the language of secrets. In a world of unstable frequencies and unstable people, their relationship is a practical channel between information and action, maintained by reputation, discounts, and the shared belief that business only works when the terms are real.]
