# Lynae

## Identity

[Lynae is the public name of a Spectro Resonator who fights with Pistols, carries the title Radiant Spectrum, and is recorded as a Congenital Resonator rather than a Synchronist. Her official student identity places her in the Preparatory Program at Startorch Academy, within the Lahai-Roi context of New Federation history, but the same record immediately complicates that identity by stating that her real name is unknown. The person called Lynae is not simply a student with a mysterious past. She is a former mercenary from the Lawless Zone who escaped that life by taking the identity of the real Lynae, a Fractsidus operative who had been attending Startorch Academy as a spy. As a result, the name on her student file is both a shield and a liability. It grants her access to the ordinary life she wants, while also tying her to a hostile organization, a stolen cover, and a past that cannot be fully erased.]

[This double identity is the center of her dossier. On paper, Lynae is a Startorch Academy student from the New Federation, affiliated with the academy and formerly with the Lawless Zone. In practice, she is someone who has crossed from mercenary survival into institutional student life by exploiting the false structure built by another person. The record does not provide her birth name, her original family background, or the precise moment when she replaced the Fractsidus spy, so her identity must be read through what is known: she wanted out of the Lawless Zone, she found a route through Startorch, and she now lives under a name that originally belonged to an enemy infiltrator. That makes her neither a clean innocent nor a simple impostor. She is a fugitive from one violent world who has hidden inside another person's conspiracy to claim a future of her own.]

## Appearance

[Lynae is described as a young woman of average height with fair skin, sharp purple-blue eyes, and light-blonde hair threaded with teal highlights. The darker brown roots still visible beneath the dyed or lightened hair give her appearance a deliberate, lived-in quality, suggesting someone who styles herself boldly without fully concealing where she came from. Her eyes are especially important to the impression she leaves: sharp, cool-toned, and alert, they fit a girl who can pass as a student while retaining the instincts of a former mercenary. Nothing in the appearance record presents her as imposing through height or armor. Instead, her visual force comes from contrast, color, and attitude.]

[Her clothing is built around a school-uniform-like outfit, which reinforces the Startorch Academy identity she now inhabits. She wears a white dress shirt, skirt, loose teal-and-green tie, and black cat tie clip, a combination that reads as playful, casual, and intentionally relaxed rather than rigidly formal. Her hair is mostly worn down, with a looped tied section and braids across her bangs and back, giving the design motion and asymmetry. The teal highlights echo the tie and help make her look vivid rather than anonymous within a school setting. Her Tacet Mark is located on the posterior side of her left thigh, a placement repeated in both the appearance and Forte records. The mark confirms her Resonator status, while its hidden location mirrors the larger pattern of her character: the most consequential truths about Lynae are present, but not immediately visible.]

## Forte

[Lynae's associated Forte is Radiant Spectrum, matching her title and her Spectro frequency. The Startorch Academy Student Records identify her through Forte Examination Report RA2906-G, where her Synchronist Qualification is marked negative and her classification is Congenital Resonator. Her Forte profile reveals a Rabelle's Curve with a pronounced periodic waveform, which is the technical basis for classifying her resonance as congenital rather than acquired through later synchronization. The academy file also notes her congenital sensitivity to visible light and an innate affinity for a term that is cut off in the source as "com," leaving that final affinity undefined. Because the available record breaks mid-word, the safest reading is that her Forte is strongly tied to light perception and Spectro expression, but that one technical detail of the profile remains incomplete.]

[The name Radiant Spectrum implies breadth rather than a single narrow beam of power. It frames Lynae as someone whose Resonance concerns visible light, color, and spectrographic variation, which aligns with the file's reference to sensitivity to visible light and with her bold habit of "painting." The source does not explain the mechanics of that painting, nor does it give a combat technique list, so the dossier should avoid inventing abilities beyond the recorded facts. What can be said is that her Forte identity is unusually coherent across title, element, weapon style, and personality. A Spectro Pistol user called Radiant Spectrum, with a documented light sensitivity and a flamboyant creative streak, is thematically built around motion, color, precision, and visibility. This is striking because her personal history depends on concealment. Her power points toward brightness and display, while her survival strategy has required secrecy, disguise, and stolen credentials.]

[The resonance stability record adds another important layer. Lynae's Rabelle's Waveform test sample shows elliptical fluctuations and a stable Time Domain, with no abnormal fluctuations observed. Her examination results are within normal parameters, her Resonant Criticality is in the normal range, subject stability remains robust, and there is no present risk of Overclocking. Psychological counseling is currently deemed unnecessary. In institutional terms, she is a remarkably safe Resonator subject. Yet the evaluator notes that the test went so smoothly, and the waveform was so textbook, that it felt a little unnatural. This does not overturn the positive diagnosis, but it gives the data an uneasy edge. Lynae's resonance is stable, perhaps almost too stable, which fits a character who has learned to present exactly the surface that systems want to see.]

## Personality

[Lynae is described as a free spirit with passion in her soul, a student who drives fast and "paints" boldly. Her dearest wish is for her trouble-free campus life to last forever. That wish is simple on the surface, but it carries unusual weight once her background is known. For many students, campus peace might be ordinary routine. For Lynae, it is the prize at the end of escape from the Lawless Zone, mercenary work, and a stolen identity connected to Fractsidus espionage. Her love of speed, color, and bold expression should therefore be read as more than youthful restlessness. It is a way of making the present feel real, vivid, and self-owned after a past defined by violence and impersonation.]

[Her personality rests on tension between recklessness and control. The public Lynae wants movement, excitement, and freedom from trouble. The hidden Lynae must constantly manage the risk that her name, past, or documents could be questioned. Her resonance record, with its nearly perfect stability, suggests that she can present composure when measured, examined, or filed. Her lifestyle details suggest that she resists becoming merely obedient to that file. She wants the academy not as a prison of rules but as a place where she can keep moving without being hunted. The result is a character who feels bright because she is pushing toward brightness, not because life has been easy.]

## History

[Lynae's known history begins before Startorch Academy, in the Lawless Zone of the New Federation. The source identifies her as a mercenary before enrollment, which means she had already lived in a world where survival, violence, and uncertain loyalties were practical realities. The Lawless Zone affiliation is explicitly marked as former, so it should not be treated as her desired present identity. It is the place she escaped, the context that made her willing to take drastic action, and the past that explains why a peaceful campus life matters so intensely to her. Her transition from mercenary to Preparatory Program student is not described as a legal transfer, a recruitment program, or a redemption ceremony. It is described through theft of identity, which gives the history a morally complicated but emotionally clear shape.]

[The decisive event in that history is her theft of the real Lynae's identity. The real Lynae was a Fractsidus operative attending Startorch Academy as a spy, and the current Lynae used that identity to get away from the Lawless Zone. The record does not state what happened to the operative, how the impersonation was carried out, or who else knows the truth. It does state enough to establish the scale of the risk. Lynae's safety depends on occupying a role built for infiltration, while her own desire is not to serve Fractsidus but to be done with the violent systems around her. Her present life at Startorch is therefore founded on an act of deception that redirects an enemy's cover toward personal escape.]

[Her history facet also preserves a fragment titled Truancy Record, saying that in Startorch Academy's administrative system, Lynae's truancy record, then the source cuts off. Even incomplete, the fragment matters because it shows that the academy does not only record her as a Resonator subject, but also as a student whose attendance behavior has become administratively visible. Combined with her free-spirited profile, fast driving, and wish for trouble-free campus life, the truancy note suggests the ordinary school problems that emerge once a former mercenary tries to inhabit a student routine. She has escaped into normalcy, but normalcy still has schedules, records, and consequences.]

## Affiliations

[Lynae's primary current affiliation is Startorch Academy, specifically its Preparatory Program. Startorch gives her shelter, structure, education, and a plausible public identity. It is also the institution that measures her Forte, records her Rabelle's Curve, evaluates her Overclocking risk, and places her in an administrative system where even truancy becomes part of the file. The academy is therefore both haven and surveillance environment. It lets her live the student life she wants, but it also produces the records that could expose inconsistencies if her assumed identity is ever scrutinized too closely.]

[Her former affiliation is the Lawless Zone, tied to her origin in the New Federation. That past is not merely geographic. It is a social and survival context, the place where she was a mercenary before she became a Startorch student. The Lawless Zone explains the urgency of her escape and the practical competence implied by her successful identity theft. Lahai-Roi supplies the current national and institutional context in which Startorch Academy operates. Through these place affiliations, Lynae's life moves from the violent margins of the New Federation into the formal academy world of Lahai-Roi.]

[Fractsidus is relevant to Lynae's dossier because the real Lynae was one of its operatives, not because the current Lynae is recorded as loyal to it. The stolen identity places her in the shadow of Fractsidus espionage, and that shadow may be more dangerous than a normal criminal past. If the organization discovers that an operative's cover has been taken over by someone else, Lynae's student life could become a liability. If Startorch discovers the same truth, her right to remain there could be threatened. Her affiliations are therefore layered and unstable: Startorch is where she belongs now, the Lawless Zone is what she fled, Lahai-Roi is the context of her new life, New Federation is the origin attached to her record, and Fractsidus is the hostile absence inside the name she wears.]
