# Zani and Cantarella

## Directional Relationship

[Zani views Cantarella from inside Ragunna's institutional and noble power structure, which makes her assessment deliberately careful. The Fisalia family has always clung to Ragunna's traditions and resisted change, at least in the common reading that Zani repeats. As a Montelli employee working within Averardo Bank, Zani is not positioned as a free political actor who can personally reshape relations between noble houses. She understands hierarchy, family interests, and the limits of her role. Her opinion of Cantarella therefore begins with restraint: she knows what the Fisalia name means, and she knows she is not the one who decides Montelli policy toward it.]

[Even so, Zani distinguishes Cantarella from the most rigid image of the Fisalia family. The current head, Signorina Cantarella, is not as traditional as people imagine. This observation matters because Zani is an orderly person, but she is not naive about order. She knows that tradition can protect stability and also imprison people. Cantarella's dossier frames her as the thirty-sixth matriarch of Fisalia, a figure bound to poisoncraft, family burden, the Sea of Dreams, and a search for liberation. Zani's comment does not romanticize that danger, but it does recognize that the woman leading the house may not be identical to the house's most stubborn habits.]

[Zani's willingness to say she would not mind working with Cantarella is quietly significant. It is not an oath of loyalty, a declaration of friendship, or a rejection of Montelli interests. It is a personal professional judgment. Zani values competence, self-possession, and people who can act within systems without being entirely consumed by them. Cantarella's composed, unconventional authority appears to meet that standard. From Zani's perspective, cooperation with such a person could be useful, perhaps even preferable to dealing with nobles who only repeat inherited positions without thought.]

[The relationship remains bounded by Zani's employee perspective. She separates personal opinion from institutional authority, saying that as an employee she does not get to influence how the Montellis handle their relationship with the Fisalias. That boundary is central to her character. Zani may carry the power of Scorched Radiance and act as a nightwalker for justice, but by day she still respects job descriptions, chains of responsibility, and the reality of noble politics. Her view of Cantarella is cautious interest: she sees danger and tradition, but also enough independence in Cantarella to imagine productive work between them if circumstances allowed.]
