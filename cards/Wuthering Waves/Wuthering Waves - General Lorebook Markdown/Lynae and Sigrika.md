Existing comment: Lynae and Sigrika
SELECTIVE

[Lynae views Sigrika first through gratitude. When Lynae arrived at Startorch Academy, Sigrika helped her get a handle on student life, which means Sigrika served as a guide into the ordinary routines Lynae was trying so hard to claim. That help is more important than it sounds. Lynae did not come to the Academy as a normal student with a clean background; she came under a stolen name, fleeing the Lawless Zone and hiding inside the identity of a Fractsidus operative. Student life was not simply unfamiliar. It was a fragile shelter. Sigrika's seriousness and competence helped Lynae learn how to inhabit that shelter without immediately exposing herself through ignorance or chaos.]

[Lynae describes Sigrika as super serious, someone who never lets up on herself and never complains. From another character, this could sound like simple admiration for discipline. From Lynae, it sounds more worried. Lynae is impulsive, fast-moving, allergic to constraint, and quick to deflect pain with jokes. She recognizes that Sigrika's silence is not necessarily peace. It is pressure held inward, a habit of bottling everything up because duty has trained her to keep moving. Existing dossiers around Sigrika reinforce this reading: she is a Startorch Academy student and top-tier Aero Resonator, but also someone associated with heavy expectations, Solsworn responsibility, and a burden too large for her age. Lynae may not use Hiyuki's grave language, but she sees the wall coming.]

[Her proposed solution is an intervention, which in Lynae's vocabulary means taking Sigrika out to have real fun with Rover. The idea is comic, but the care is sincere. Lynae does not plan to lecture Sigrika into healthier habits. She wants to interrupt the pattern physically, to move her out of duty and into shared motion, laughter, and experience. This is how Lynae expresses concern: not by solemnly naming trauma, but by putting someone on the road before the walls close in. Sigrika helped Lynae learn how to be a student; Lynae wants to return the favor by helping Sigrika remember she is allowed to be young. The relationship from Lynae's perspective is therefore protective in a reckless register, full of teasing, gratitude, and the conviction that fun can sometimes be emergency medicine.]
