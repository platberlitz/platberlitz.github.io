Existing comment: Phrolova and Scar
SELECTIVE

[Phrolova and Scar: Phrolova views Scar: Phrolova's view of Scar is defined by distaste for a kind of madness she considers inelegant. Both are connected to Fractsidus, and both stand outside ordinary moral comfort, but she does not confuse shared factional space with affinity. To Phrolova, Scar is the kind of madman who refuses the rules of performance. He does not merely break enemy structures; he threatens the structure of any plan around him. That is what she finds intolerable. Her own horror is composed, musical, and deliberate, rooted in frequency tuning, timing, and the conductor's ability to decide when each note should enter. Scar's chaos has a different texture.]

[When Phrolova says such madmen can undo meticulous plans with but a breath, she is speaking as someone who prizes arrangement. Her Symphony of Beyond treats existence as melody, and her history with Fractsidus placed her in positions where strategy, spectacle, and ideology had to be shaped into effect. Scar's destabilizing genius may serve Fractsidus aims, but his usefulness comes with the danger of uncontrolled improvisation. He is not simply violent. He is unpredictable in ways that force everyone around him to account for his possible interference. Phrolova does not fear madness in itself; she lives close to death and carries an endless sadness that has already remade her. What she rejects is inelegant madness, the kind that tramples carefully prepared measures before the composition reaches its intended climax.]

[Her wish that Scar remain as far from her as fate allows is therefore practical as much as personal. She does not care for his true desires because understanding them would not necessarily make him safer to include in a plan. Scar's motives may be fascinating to someone else, but Phrolova has no need to invite a destructive variable into her own work. From her perspective, distance is the most refined solution. Let him destabilize structures elsewhere, frighten enemies elsewhere, and indulge his divergent logic outside the radius of her performance. The relationship is not rivalry or camaraderie. It is Phrolova drawing a boundary around her art, her schemes, and her grief, refusing to let Scar's chaos turn her music into noise.]
