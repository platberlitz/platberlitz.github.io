# Qiuyuan

## Identity

[Qiuyuan is a Huanglong Resonator of Aero frequency who wields a Sword and carries the title Bambooscape, a name that binds his public image to quiet discipline, natural resilience, and the cutting grace of a wandering swordsman. His Resonator classification is listed as unclear, which makes him a figure of official interest as well as martial respect, but the available records are consistent about the shape of his life: he is not a seeker of fame, rank, or courtly reward. He once served as a senior agent of Mingting's Internal Security Agency, placing him inside the security apparatus that operates around Mingting and Chongzhou, yet he now wanders Huanglong alone. His origin and nation are both Huanglong, and his personal path runs through the administrative capital, the province of Chongzhou, and the agency he formerly served. Qiuyuan's identity is therefore divided between public service and private exile. He is not portrayed as a renegade chasing notoriety, but as a man whose sense of justice outlasted his position. The core of his character is absolute integrity, cool resilience, and an almost severe commitment to moral cultivation through swordsmanship.]

## Appearance

[Qiuyuan's appearance reinforces the impression of a disciplined martial traveler rather than a court official or professional soldier bound to uniform. He is a tall, muscular man with muted gray eyes, a straight nose, and a clean scar along his left cheek, giving his face a restrained severity that suggests hardship endured without theatrical display. His long dark hair is tipped in light gray and tied into a ponytail with an emerald string and pin, falling between his shoulder blades when bound. The detail is elegant but practical, like much else about him, balancing refinement with readiness. His Tacet Mark sits on his right palm, a significant placement for a swordsman, since the hand that grips, strikes, blocks, and measures distance also bears the visible sign of Resonance. His rice hat, flowing garments accented with green, and lean sword bearing create the silhouette of a wandering martial artist who belongs as much to mountain paths and bamboo shade as to the records of ministries. Nothing in the description is gaudy. The scar, hat, tied hair, and green accents all point toward someone whose presence is quiet, controlled, and unmistakable once seen.]

## Forte

[Qiuyuan's associated Forte or power is Bambooscape, though the official records are more revealing in what they cannot say than in what they can. The Ministry of Sentinel Affairs received documents from the Internal Security Agency, the Justice Commission, and the Huaxu Academy, then instructed the Chongzhou administrator to assist by examining the local Resonator register. That investigation confirmed that Qiuyuan has no connection with either the Sentinel or the Threnodian of Chongzhou, ruling out two obvious explanations for his unusual file. Later correspondence from the Ministry of Sentinel Affairs describes him as reserved about the nature of his ability and suggests that he does not simply refuse to employ his Forte, but consciously restrains himself from using it. This distinction matters. Refusal could imply fear, ignorance, or stubbornness, while restraint implies knowledge, discipline, and deliberate ethical control. The Academy report records an elliptical oscillation in his waveform analysis, stable time domain readings, and no anomalous patterns. His Resonant Criticality is high, but his stability is also high, with no risk noted. Qiuyuan's power therefore appears potent, carefully contained, and technically stable, a Forte that he keeps sheathed in the same spirit as his sword until purpose and principle require otherwise.]

## Personality

[Qiuyuan is defined by cool resilience and absolute integrity. The records repeatedly emphasize that he wanders alone and seeks neither fame nor high office, a formulation that reads less like romantic detachment and more like a considered rejection of corrupting incentives. His ambition is not social elevation, but mastery of swordsmanship and a life guided by high morals. This gives him a severe but not cold temperament. He is disciplined enough to restrain his Forte, strong enough to survive the loss of official standing, and principled enough that his moral code remains central even after institutional betrayal. The phrase Bambooscape suits this personality, because bamboo bends without surrendering its rootedness, grows straight, and survives weather through flexibility rather than brute resistance. Qiuyuan's resilience has that same quality. He can leave Mingting's security structure behind without losing the habits of duty that service impressed upon him. He can continue walking through Huanglong without converting hardship into bitterness. His solitude is not emptiness, but chosen austerity. It is the space in which he sharpens his sword, tests his judgment, and keeps his conduct aligned with the standards he refuses to abandon.]

## History

[Qiuyuan's history begins in the structures of Huanglong authority, where Mingting stands as the capital and the seat of ministries, political institutions, Sentinel affairs, justice, and security. He served within that world as a senior agent of the Internal Security Agency, an organization responsible for security affairs around Mingting and Chongzhou. Such a rank implies competence, trust, and a record of service before his fall. The decisive rupture came when he was framed for his master's death, turning the agency from a former duty into a burden from his past. The available dossier does not identify the true culprit or describe the full political circumstances, but the consequences are clear: Qiuyuan moved from institutional agent to solitary wanderer, carrying both the discipline of his former profession and the wound of wrongful accusation. His history entry preserves a hard saying about mountains as trials, where passing grants one the freedom to follow one's own path, while failing means the mountain keeps what one cannot bring beyond it. That idea fits the man who emerges from the records. Qiuyuan treats life as a sequence of trials that reveal the weight and worth of a person's convictions. He has been tested by office, accusation, exile, and power, yet the record still describes him first as a swordsman of integrity, not as a victim of intrigue. His past explains his burden, but it does not consume his identity.]

## Affiliations

[Qiuyuan's affiliations connect him to Mingting, Chongzhou, and the Internal Security Agency formerly, all within the broader context of Huanglong. Mingting is the capital of Huanglong and the administrative center where ministries and political institutions converge, so his former service placed him close to the machinery of state authority. Chongzhou is equally important to his file, because the Ministry of Sentinel Affairs ordered local administrative assistance there when his Resonator status and possible connections were examined. The Internal Security Agency remains his most defining formal affiliation, even though it is explicitly former. It represents the life in which he served as a senior agent, handled security concerns tied to the region, and likely developed the investigative discipline and composure that still shape him. Yet it also represents the injustice that forced him away from official service after he was framed for his master's death. Qiuyuan's relationship with institutions is therefore complex. He is not outside Huanglong's world because he lacks loyalty to it, but because loyalty to justice and moral conduct can survive separation from the offices that once claimed to embody those values. His true affiliation now appears to be with the road, the sword, and the ethical demands he places upon himself as he continues through Huanglong alone.]
