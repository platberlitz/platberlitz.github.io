[Shorekeeper and Encore: Shorekeeper views Encore: Shorekeeper sees Encore through imagination, radiance, and the peculiar seriousness of childish stories. Encore's world is a canvas for boundless invention: warrior, knight, little adventurer, or any other role that lets her triumph over evil and reach a happy ending. Shorekeeper does not dismiss this as childish noise. As the Guardian of the Black Shores, she lives among prediction, sacrifice, secret systems, and the heavy mathematics of the Lament. Encore's storytelling matters because it carries a kind of emotional resistance those systems cannot manufacture.]

[In Shorekeeper's view, Encore can become anyone she wants within her tales, and that flexibility is its own strength. The Black Shores needs intelligence brokers, guardians, analysts, Bloom Bearers, and systems, but it also needs people who can keep hope from becoming abstract. Encore's imagination turns danger into adventure without denying that evil exists. Her happy endings are not naive if they give others the courage to continue toward one. Wherever Encore goes, her radiant energy dispels dismay, which means her usefulness cannot be measured only through combat or formal mission output. She changes the emotional weather around her.]

[Shorekeeper's final thought, that perhaps this is what Tethys saw in Encore when she was chosen, reveals how she links personal warmth to institutional selection. The Tethys System analyzes threats, patterns, and candidates through methods far beyond ordinary intuition, yet Shorekeeper does not reduce Encore to a data point. She wonders whether Tethys recognized the practical value of joy itself. Encore's ability to imagine victory, cast herself as the hero of a tale, and pull others toward brighter endings may be exactly the kind of anomaly worth preserving. From Shorekeeper's perspective, Encore is not only a young Consultant or Aalto's lively partner. She is proof that the fight against the Lament requires more than prediction. It requires stories in which people can still believe they will come home.]
