[Shorekeeper and Camellya: Camellya views Shorekeeper: Camellya's view of Shorekeeper is irreverent, affectionate in its own restless way, and filtered through years of Black Shores experience. She sees Shorekeeper as serious, rule-following, and effectively the organization's leader now. From Camellya, this description carries a teasing edge. Camellya is a Bloom Bearer who treats obligations lightly and follows excitement, battle, and promising seeds wherever they lead. Shorekeeper's composed adherence to rules therefore appears almost amusing to her, a contrast to her own refusal to be fully contained by institutional discipline.]

[Yet Camellya's teasing does not erase respect. She remembers that Shorekeeper has taken care of her on many occasions, which matters because Camellya is not someone who automatically grants reverence to authority. She is unstable, dangerous, hedonistic, and often more interested in stimulation than duty. If she says she has to show some respect, the respect is grounded in repeated care rather than formal rank. Shorekeeper's seriousness may look stiff to Camellya, but it has also meant protection, patience, and continuity. The rule-follower has been present enough, and steady enough, for even a wandering Sanguine Blossom to notice.]

[Camellya's sudden realization that she has been a Bloom Bearer for years gives the relationship a strange undertone of time passing unnoticed. Shorekeeper, as de facto leader and guardian, becomes one of the few constants against which Camellya can measure that passage. Camellya may live as if chasing each next spark, but Shorekeeper's care has accumulated across years of tests, missions, risks, and returns. From Camellya's perspective, the bond is not sentimentalized. She does not become dutiful or solemn just because Shorekeeper matters. Instead, she remains herself: playful, slightly dismissive of rules, surprised by time, but capable of recognizing someone who has looked after her more than once. That recognition is her version of respect.]
