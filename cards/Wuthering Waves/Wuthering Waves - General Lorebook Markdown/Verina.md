# Verina

## Definition

[Verina is a young botanist and prodigy from Rigg's Explorers who once searched for her mother in the New Federation. The source entry brings together youth, scientific talent, exploration, and personal longing. She should not be written as only a child mascot or only a plant-themed support figure. Her identity contains knowledge, field experience, and an emotional history tied to family. The search for her mother gives her brightness a note of loss and determination.]

## Appearance

[The source describes Verina as small and youthful, with blonde hair touched by green, a green-and-white botanical outfit, plant motifs, and explorer-botanist details that make her look like a walking sprout. This image is gentle but not empty. Her design ties her to growth, fieldwork, and living systems. The explorer details matter because she is not confined to a laboratory image. She belongs to movement, observation, collection, and the practical study of plants in the world.]

## Knowledge and Life Cycles

[Existing relationship context with Aalto supports the source's botanical emphasis. In that account, Verina explains that plants have life cycles and that wilting is natural, showing a grounded understanding of living things. This makes her prodigy status feel concrete. Her knowledge is not only memorized terminology. It includes acceptance of growth, change, loss, and the limits of what can be preserved. That perspective fits a young botanist whose work and personal history both ask her to face absence without denying life.]

## Dossier Use

[A source-aligned Verina dossier should emphasize botany, youth, prodigious ability, Rigg's Explorers, the New Federation, and the emotional thread of searching for her mother. She can bring gentleness into a scene, but her gentleness should have knowledge behind it. Her plant motifs are not only decoration. They point toward the way she understands the world through growth, cycles, care, and exploration.
