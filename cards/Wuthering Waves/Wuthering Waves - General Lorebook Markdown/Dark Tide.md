## The Nature of the Catastrophe

[The Dark Tide is a recurring phenomenon of corruption and sea-born disaster that afflicts the region of Rinascita, representing one of the most devastating and poorly understood threats on Solaris-3. It manifests as a massive wave of destructive resonance energy that emerges from the sea, corrupting everything it touches and leaving devastation in its wake. The Dark Tide is not merely a natural disaster but carries with it a quality of corruption, transforming the environments and entities it contacts into something alien and hostile. Its connection to Leviathan, the Threnodian associated with Rinascita's history, suggests that the Dark Tide may be an expression of something far more sentient and purposeful than a mere environmental catastrophe.]

## Two-Faced History

[The history of the Dark Tide is described as two-faced, suggesting that the official narrative maintained by institutions like the Order of the Deep differs significantly from the truth of what has occurred. This duality may reflect deliberate suppression of knowledge about the Dark Tide's true nature, competing interpretations of historical events, or the fundamental ambiguity of a phenomenon that defies easy categorization. The institutions tasked with responding to and explaining the Dark Tide face an impossible balancing act between providing enough information to enable effective response and maintaining enough mystery to avoid panic or the erosion of their own authority.]

## Cartethyia's Impossible Burden

[The Dark Tide's history connects directly to Cartethyia, who carries both Sentinel and Threnodian legacy inside her, making her a living embodiment of the conflict the Dark Tide represents. As the former Blessed Maiden and vessel of Divinity known as Fleurdelys, she was positioned as the prophesied answer to the Dark Tide threat. Her subsequent departure from that role to become a wandering knight may reflect her refusal to serve as a weapon in a conflict whose true nature she has come to understand. The impossible burden she carries is both personal and cosmic, the weight of being a being who contains contradictory legacies and who must navigate a destiny shaped by forces that view her as a tool rather than a person.]
