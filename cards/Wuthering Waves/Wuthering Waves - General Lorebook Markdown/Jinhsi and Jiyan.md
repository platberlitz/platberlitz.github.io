Existing comment: Jinhsi and Jiyan
SELECTIVE

[Jinhsi views Jiyan through trust strong enough to become civic faith. The nursery rhyme she recalls, Qingloong rise, monsters gone, is known by every child in Jinzhou. That detail matters because Jiyan's protection is not only a military fact in her mind. It has entered the city's shared language, the simple phrases children learn before they can understand the full terror of Tacet Discords or the labor required to keep them beyond the borders. As Magistrate, Jinhsi must think in reports, deployments, resources, and public morale. As a citizen of Jinzhou, she also understands the comfort of a name that lets children imagine monsters being driven away.]

[Her faith rests with Jiyan because his protection allows Jinzhou's fragile order to hold. The city is a frontier settlement in Huanglong, guarded by Sentinel Jué but still exposed to wilderness threats, border pressure, and the long consequences of the Lament. Jinhsi carries the city's political and spiritual obligations, yet even she cannot personally stand at every border. Jiyan, as the general associated with Jinzhou's military defense and the Midnight Rangers, gives her leadership a shield beyond City Hall. His composure, command experience, and record of protecting the frontier make civic hope materially possible.]

[This relationship remains Jinhsi's perspective rather than a full biography of Jiyan. She does not describe him with strategic detachment alone. She speaks as a ruler who needs to believe that Tacet Discords will not breach her people's home, and as a young leader humble enough to place trust in those whose duties differ from her own. That trust is not blind. It is built from reputation, repeated defense, and the evidence of a city still standing. Jinhsi's faith in Jiyan shows her ability to distribute responsibility without surrendering care. She carries Jinzhou's future, but she knows that future depends on guardians at the border, and in Jiyan she sees one whose rising Qingloong image still keeps monsters from the gate.]
