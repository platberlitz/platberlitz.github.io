SELECTIVE

[The source direction for this entry is Hiyuki viewing Lynae, so the relationship should preserve Hiyuki's careful, protective gaze even while the file remains keyed under Lynae. Hiyuki sees Lynae as a bright spirit, and that phrase matters because it comes from someone whose own life is defined by severe duty, vanished Ashinohara, the Miko of Eternal Flaming Sakura, and the long burden of entrusted wishes. Hiyuki does not mistake Lynae's speed, jokes, and truancy-adjacent mischief for emptiness. She recognizes brightness as something Lynae is moving toward with urgency, perhaps because Lynae's past did not naturally provide it.]

[Hiyuki is aware of certain secrets Lynae carries. The dossier should not turn that awareness into a full public accusation, but the surrounding lore clarifies the kind of weight involved: Lynae is a former mercenary from the Lawless Zone who escaped by taking the identity of the real Lynae, a Fractsidus operative enrolled at Startorch Academy as a spy. Hiyuki's comment that they are both bound by their pasts creates a quiet bridge between very different histories. Hiyuki is bound by sacred office, Suzu, Ashinohara's loss, and truths that made her answer with silence and bitter liquor. Lynae is bound by a stolen name, survival deception, and the fear that her peaceful campus life could be taken away if the wrong person sees too clearly.]

[What dominates Hiyuki's view is hope rather than suspicion. She believes Lynae has a future she is galloping toward, one filled with light and hope that Lynae wholly deserves. That is a powerful judgment from a woman who has seen how duty and history can narrow a life. Hiyuki's protection is not permissiveness, however. Her dry note that Lynae should avoid using her Forte to slip into class when late brings the relationship back to ordinary student discipline. Hiyuki can hold Lynae's secrets in one hand and her attendance problems in the other, treating both as part of the same living person. In that sense, Hiyuki's perspective grants Lynae something precious: the right to be understood deeply without being reduced to danger, and the right to be scolded for normal teenage irresponsibility because a future at Startorch still belongs to her.]
