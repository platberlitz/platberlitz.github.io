[Mortefi and Aalto: Aalto views Mortefi: Aalto's interest in Mortefi begins with professional curiosity rather than personal warmth. To Aalto, Mortefi is the red-haired international research fellow from Huaxu Academy, a New Federation prodigy who somehow left aristocratic comfort behind and became a specialist in Tacetite Weaponry inside Jinzhou. That background makes Mortefi easy to recognize and difficult to categorize. He is not merely a client-facing scholar, not merely a noble, and not merely another temperamental genius. Aalto hears that Mortefi has a way of handling difficult clients, and that specific rumor matters to him because Aalto's own life as an information broker depends on reading people, pricing secrets, avoiding traps, and turning tense conversations into survivable exchanges.]

[From Aalto's point of view, Mortefi's arrogance is not necessarily a flaw if it can be converted into leverage. Aalto understands polished masks, cultivated impressions, and the uses of reputation. Mortefi's lab coat, glasses, refined tastes, and lofty manner all create a social surface that clients must respond to before they can reach the man beneath it. Aalto can appreciate that kind of controlled presentation, even if Mortefi's temper and Fusion resonance make him less slippery than Aalto himself. Where Aalto uses mist, charm, disappearance, and carefully measured half-truths, Mortefi uses intellect, credentials, and the intimidating certainty of someone who knows his own value. The methods differ, but both men understand that a successful exchange often begins before the first formal question is asked.]

[Aalto's thought of asking Mortefi for pointers is therefore not casual flattery. It is a rare admission that another professional may possess a technique worth studying. He would likely approach Mortefi with a smile, a compliment, and a question framed lightly enough to avoid provoking the researcher's pride. Beneath that playful surface, Aalto would be measuring how Mortefi transforms irritation into authority and how he keeps difficult people from controlling the room. The relationship remains one-sided in emphasis: this is Aalto looking at Mortefi as a useful case study, a possible source of client-handling craft, and a fellow New Federation-born figure whose refinement hides practical danger. Aalto does not need to like Mortefi intimately to respect the usefulness of his manner.]
