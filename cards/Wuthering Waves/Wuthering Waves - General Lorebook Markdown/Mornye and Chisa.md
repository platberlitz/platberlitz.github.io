Existing comment: Mornye and Chisa
SELECTIVE

[Mornye views Chisa through a rare continuity of memory across catastrophe. To Mornye, Chisa is not only the quiet Ashinoharan student currently writing the best papers in her class. She is also the freshman girl who vanished in the Void Storm, an incident Mornye remembers clearly because it happened during her first year as a teaching assistant at Startorch Academy. That chronology matters. Mornye was already inside the Academy's educational structure when Chisa's disappearance became part of its history, and she remembers not a vague name from a casualty list, but a student whose work passed through her own hands.]

[The paper Mornye graded becomes the anchor of her perspective. She describes it as exceptionally well-written, with logical structure, rigorous analysis, and precise formatting. These are not casual compliments. They are exactly the details a quiet, exacting professor of Exostrider Engineering would value. Mornye's intelligence is disciplined, technical, and oriented toward the mapping of difficult realities. When she praises Chisa's structure, rigor, and formatting, she is recognizing a mind that can order complexity. Chisa's broader identity supports this assessment: she perceives hidden structures beneath the surface of reality, seeing threads that connect cause, effect, life, death, and fate. Mornye may be speaking as a teacher grading a paper, but her praise aligns with Chisa's deepest ability.]

[The emotional force of the relationship lies in the phrase now. Chisa vanished in the Void Storm, yet now she is the student who writes the best papers in Mornye's class. For Mornye, a former Startorch student and present professor shaped by Lahai-Roi's post-disaster world, this is more than academic satisfaction. It is evidence of continuity after rupture. A student once marked by disappearance has returned to the classroom as a mind of exceptional precision. Mornye's tone remains restrained, as suits her personality, but the restraint does not erase the feeling. She remembers the incident, remembers the paper, and measures the present student against that old fragment. Chisa's excellence is therefore doubled: she is brilliant now, and her brilliance reaches back across the Void Storm to prove that the person Mornye once glimpsed in a paper was not lost entirely.]
