## Overview

[Rabelle's Curve is a classification framework used in formal Resonator examinations to assess and categorize the nature and stability of a Resonator's Awakening. Named after its originator, the framework provides a standardized method for evaluating how far a Resonator's frequency profile deviates from baseline human norms, enabling organizations to identify potential risks and assign appropriate classifications.]

## Classification Categories

[The framework organizes Resonators into several key categories. The Normal category encompasses Resonators whose frequency output, Forte expression, and Tacet Mark behavior fall within expected parameters. These individuals demonstrate stable control and predictable power scaling. The Abnormal category includes Resonators whose readings deviate meaningfully from the curve without posing immediate danger, often indicating unusual Forte expressions, atypical resonance patterns, or heightened sensitivity to external frequencies. The Mutation category flags Resonators whose profiles suggest active instability, unpredictable evolution of their Forte, or signs that their frequency output may be approaching dangerous thresholds associated with Overclocking.]

## Practical Applications

[Organizations such as the Black Shores, the Huaxu Academy, and regional militias use Rabelle's Curve readings during recruitment, assignment, and ongoing monitoring. A Resonator classified as Normal might be assigned to routine patrol or support duties, while Abnormal classifications often warrant specialized roles or closer supervision. Mutation classifications trigger intensive monitoring and sometimes confinement, as the risks of uncontrolled Overclocking or Forte destabilization threaten not only the Resonator but everyone in proximity. The Midnight Rangers in Jinzhou and the various sentinel orders of Rinascita all maintain Rabelle's Curve records for their members.]

## Limitations

[Rabelle's Curve is a useful tool but not infallible. Some Resonators, particularly those with unique or unprecedented Forte types, defy clean categorization. The Rover, for example, possesses a frequency profile that shifts between Spectro, Havoc, and Aero expressions, confounding standard examination protocols. Additionally, Rabelle's Curve captures a snapshot in time and may not predict sudden changes in a Resonator's condition brought on by trauma, emotional distress, or exposure to Lament-adjacent phenomena.]
